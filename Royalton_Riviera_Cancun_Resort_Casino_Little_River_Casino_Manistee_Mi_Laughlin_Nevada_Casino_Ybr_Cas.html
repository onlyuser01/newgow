The provider of content, technology and solutions, powering iGaming operators across the globe, EGT Digital, announced a new Asia-facing partnership. The company confirmed this week it expanded its reach to Armenia thanks to a deal with the leading betting operator, Vbet. Under the terms of the freshly minted content agreement, EGT Digital agreed to supply games, powering the operator's site www.vbet.am.

Thanks to the new collaboration, Armenian customers can now benefit from quality games provided by the leading iGaming content supplier. Expanding its offering, Vbet anticipates growing further its market share and engaging with new clients. At the same time, the collaboration propels EGT Digital's reach, enabling the iGaming supplier's thrilling games to reach new customers.

Ruzanna Elchyan, Vbet's Head of Gaming, revealed that the company is thrilled to join forces with the Bulgarian iGaming content supplier. Sharing her excitement in a statement, she explained: "EGT Digital's games have been demonstrating an excellent performance so far and are our customers' favorites." Finally, Elchyan pointed out: "I believe that they will attract many new visitors to our site and will facilitate the strengthening of our leadership market positions."

Per the new collaboration, Vbet's customers will gain access to games from EGT Digital such as High Cash and Bell Link. Moreover, they will be able to tap into Single Progressive Jackpot and Clover Chance, among other quality slot games from the fast-growing iGaming content provider. EGT Digital's multiplayer game, xRide, is also available for Vbet customers, granting a chance for winnings to be tracked in real-time by the participants through an increasing multiplier.

Tsvetomira Drumeva, EGT Digital's Head of Sales, commented: "We are very happy that Vbet are now among our partners." Last but not least, she said: "The good results of our joint work are already a fact and I am confident that this is only the beginning of a stable long-term collaboration, thanks to which we will provide local players with access to first-class gaming content."
