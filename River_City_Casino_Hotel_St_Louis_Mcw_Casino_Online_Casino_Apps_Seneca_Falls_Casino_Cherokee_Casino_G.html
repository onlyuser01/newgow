NYX – the digital arm of Scientific Games with a big reputation for quality gaming content

NYX Interactive was formed in Stockholm in 2006 with the aim of delivering gaming titles and software to an international market. It certainly met this aim, implementing a successful strategy and winning the trust of hundreds of customers.

A cornerstone of this success has been forming the NYX Gaming Group with NextGen; a partnership that landed them multiple prestigious EGR awards for its Open Gaming System software – first in 2013, and then in 2016, along with a sports betting award for recently-acquired subsidiary OpenBet.

NYX by this point was one of the leading suppliers in its market with an extensive library of desktop and mobile games, and a workforce of well over 1,000 people across five continents.

Perhaps it was no surprise, then, that interest in the company picked up significantly – to such an extent that the world-famous Scientific Games started to pursue a takeover deal in late 2017. By the turn of the year, a deal worth $630 million had been agreed, and the NYX Gaming Group became the Scientific Games' new interactive division, also known as SGI.

Today, NYX, under the SGI brand, has a bold ambition – to be the world leader in digital gaming, sports betting and iLottery, according to its website. It also backs up its content with a range of certificates, such as the coveted Maltese Gaming Authority license, so players should be confident that it follows the strict regulations set out by the licence.
