LAFC Hoping to End Its Losing Streak

Los Angeles FC is 11-7-9 this season, and with 40 points, the winners of last season’s MLS Cup are sitting in the No. 3 position. Steve Cherundolo’s men don’t have to worry too much about getting to the playoffs, but there are some reasons for concern. The biggest one is that they’ve lost three games in a row.

To be fair to them, they did play against Inter Miami and Charlotte FC in two of those. They also lost to the Portland Timbers, but in that game, they were without five of their first-team players who were away on international duty. LA Galaxy Is Desperate for Points

Los Angeles Galaxy is second from the bottom in the Western Conference. Greg Vanney’s boys are 7-9-10 with 30 points. That’s just four fewer than the closest team from the top 9, meaning that they still have a decent chance of getting to the postseason.
