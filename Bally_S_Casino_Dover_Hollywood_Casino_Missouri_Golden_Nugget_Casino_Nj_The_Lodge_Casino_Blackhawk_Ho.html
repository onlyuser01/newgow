PGA Tour and FanDuel to Take on North Carolina Together

Meanwhile, North Carolina has made it possible for sports betting operators to partner up with local leagues and franchises to enter the regulated market which is due to get underway in 2024. FanDuel will naturally will not be the only company that makes its mark locally, with other confirmed operators such as Caesars, DraftKings, and BetMGM all gearing up for a launch.

Apart from these established companies, there will be up-and-coming new operators, to wit Fanatics and ESPN Bet which are set to pose a major challenge to established gambling giants and have even elicited a fearful response from the likes of BetMGM and DraftKings.

When mobile sports gambling will launch is yet to be determined, as most companies do not expect this to happen on time for March Madness, which is one of the biggest wagering events of the year, and certainly not for the Super Bowl. Mobile wagering should be up and running for the next NFL regular season, however.

The PGA Tour also hosts several events out of North Carolina and its relationship with FanDuel will allow for more fan-focused sports betting experiences as well. Among the events to be expected hosted out of the Tar Heel State are such PGA Tour stops as the Wells Fargo Championship in May.

Building off an existing partner made it a pretty easy conversation to get to the parameters of a deal that we were very comfortable with,” Genetski said.
