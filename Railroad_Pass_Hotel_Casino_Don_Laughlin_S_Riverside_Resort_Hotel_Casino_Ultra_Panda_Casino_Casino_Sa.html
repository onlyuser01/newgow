The Supreme Court of Lithuania ruled in favor of the defendant in a case involving poker training. It found the program to be a form gambling promotion and declared it not in the public interest because gambling is dangerous to society.

The plaintiff and the defendant had entered into a contract whereby the former would teach them poker and finance any games. The agreement provided that profits would be shared and losses to be repaid to plaintiffs if the defendant loses any games. The plaintiff demanded payment after the defendant lost a match. The Supreme Court, however, ruled in favor of the defendant.

The Supreme Court said of its decision, "After examining the case, the Supreme Court of Lithuania pointed out that a clear legal policy regarding gambling is being formed in Lithuania - such activities are not encouraged and considered dangerous to society; therefore, gambling is restricted and controlled by various legal acts.

"The Supreme Court of Lithuania, finding that the agreement promoted gambling and that the plaintiffs sought to involve the defendant in gambling, declared the agreement contrary to public policy (i.e., the public interest), and dismissed the action."
