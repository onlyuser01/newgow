Should Arsenal’s Recent Dip in Form Worry Mikel Arteta?

The former assistant of Pep Guardiola has done a marvelous job at Arsenal. As the Gunners coach, Mikel Arteta has turned this relatively inexperienced bunch into world-beaters. They started the 2022-23 season in top gear, getting to the No. 1 spot straight away. They’re still standing there, but the question is for how long. Can they stay on top of the Prem until the end of the season?

A recent slip in their form has made their fans worry whether Man City can catch up with them. Right now, the point difference is just three points in Arsenal’s favor. The Gunners do have one game in hand, but their recent form isn’t giving their fans optimism that they won’t fail again.

They got one loss and one draw in their last two Premier League games, which is strange as they played against mediocre teams, Everton and Brentford. One possible explanation is that they have problems with injuries. One of their key men in the first half of the season, Gabriel Jesus, remains sidelined, but he was out before as well and they didn’t mind.

A Must-Win Scenario for Pep Guardiola’s Boys

Manchester City have dropped too many points this season and there’s no room for more if they want to stay in the race for the title. The reigning champions are sitting in second place, with a 15-3-4 record, which isn’t too bad, but it’s just not something they got us used to.

Their biggest problem this season has been their form on the road. In away matches, they’re 5-2-3, with two straight losses (to Tottenham and Manchester United). This time, however, they mustn’t let the same happen.

Luckily for Pep Guardiola, he’s got some elite players at his disposal. Not only do they have talent, but they also have plenty of experience playing important games such as the one scheduled for Wednesday.
