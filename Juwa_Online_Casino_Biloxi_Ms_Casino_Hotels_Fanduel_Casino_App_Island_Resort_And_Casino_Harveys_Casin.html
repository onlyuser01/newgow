SportPesa Kenya: Is It Really Back?

SportPesa returned to Kenya on October 30, 2020, after taking a leave of absence in 2019. The reason behind the withdrawal was a dispute with the government. The Betting Control and Licensing Board (BCLB) suspended the company’s license in July 2019, arguing that it owed millions in back tax.

That tax was supposedly calculated on a proposed by the government’s decision to tax the gross revenue receipts, which would have put SportPesa at a severe financial disadvantage and inability to operate. SportPesa Kenya decided to leave the country instead and pulled the plug on several high-profile sports partnerships.

The sportsbook did defeat the controversial tax law with the help of Nairobi High Court, but when the brand returned in 2020, it was once again blocked by the Betting Control and Licensing Board, which argued that the new operator of the trademark, Milestone Games Limited, had not obtained it as per the required regulatory framework.

Shortly before SportPesa returned in October 2020, the government had said it would consider enabling mobile financial transactions for the purposes of gambling. The Kenyan government also made some concessions in July 2020 when it agreed to overturn the controversial tax betting law with the 2020 Finance Bill.
