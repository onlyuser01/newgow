Infromation about ClickandBuy casinos

ClickandBuy was a German e-money payment provider and digital wallet that was a subsidiary of Deutsche Telekom. The company discontinued its service in April 2016, which means it is now impossible to use it to make online casino deposits and withdrawals.

The decision to halt ClickandBuy's service came as a shock to its many consumers when the decision was announced. Up until that point, it had appeared to be a successful enterprise. Like many similar services, ClickandBuy relied on ease-of-use, speed, and security, and appeared to be good at all three. Users simply registered with their bank account or credit card info free of charge, and could then transfer money to merchants or individuals by entering their login details.

ClickandBuy also had a few nice features. Users could set monthly spending limits, and in 2012 the company allowed customers to make purchases via their mobile, a relatively new service at the time.

But perhaps the warning signs were there to see. After a history of security lapses, journalist Kristina Beer reported another 'hole' in ClickandBuy's security armor in 2013. Perhaps because of this, or maybe because of the increased competition in digital providers, the company suddenly announced it would discontinue its service in April 2016, without giving a reason.

According to the company website, customers had until six years after the termination date – April 2022 – to claim any funds still in their ClickandBuy account.
