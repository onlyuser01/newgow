The famous research company has surveyed more than 27 million employees within 70,000 different companies over the last 16 years. The anonymous survey "uniquely measures 15 culture drivers that are critical to the success of any organization, including: alignment, execution, and connection, among others," a statement released by Everi reveals.

Eric Rubino, Energage's CEO, shared his excitement about the latest announcement saying: "Earning a Top Workplaces award is a badge of honor for companies, especially because it comes authentically from their employees." He outlined that companies that are honored with the award should be proud.

Finally, Rubino pointed out: "In today's market, leaders must ensure they're allowing employees to have a voice and be heard. That's paramount. Top Workplaces do this, and it pays dividends."

The recognition comes at a time when Everi secured four "Culture Excellence" awards from Top Workplaces earlier this year. The company was recognized for its Compensation and Benefits, Leadership, Work-Life Flexibility and Innovation only recently. Now, the new recognition further complements Everi's leadership position as a top employer.

Randy Taylor, Everi's CEO, shared his excitement about the latest achievement. He said: "We have worked diligently to create a positive company culture with shared values across multiple office locations and with our talented team of remote employees in Nevada, so we are thrilled to be recognized once again by Top Workplaces."
