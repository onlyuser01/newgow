Monterrey Determined to Show Liga MX is Better Than MLS

Monterrey is the only Mexican team left in the 2023 Leagues Cup, but can these guys go all the way? They’ve been brilliant since the start of the tournament, winning every single game in regulation. The most impressive thing is that Monterrey has faced some real heavyweights of North American soccer so far.

After a relatively easy job in the group phase – against Real Salt Lake, Seattle Sounders, and Portland Timbers – Monterrey played against two very tough opponents in the knockout stage of the Leagues Cup.

First, “Rayados” played against the champions of the 2023 Mexican Liga MX Clausura, UANL Tigres. It was a very close game, but Monterrey won 1-0 thanks to the goal from the penalty spot by its new playmaker Sergio Canales in the 7th minute of stoppage time.

Then, in the quarterfinal of the 2023 Leagues Cup, Monterrey defeated the champion of the MLS, Los Angeles FC. It was another incredibly tense game, which saw the Mexicans pull back from 2-0 down to win 3-2.

Nashville SC Fans Putting Their Hopes in Hany Mukhtar

Hany Mukhtar is one of the best players in the MLS, and the biggest star of Nashville SC. The playmaker put on a vintage show in the 5-0 demolition of Minnesota United in the quarterfinal, which saw him score one goal and make three assists.

He was also very good against Club America in the round of 16, in which his team won by penalties after 2-2 in regulation. With him in top form, the “Boys in Gold” look capable of scoring against literally any opponent in the Leagues Cup.

However, Nashville SC just isn’t great at the back. These guys have conceded eight goals so far in the Leagues Cup. Plus, in their last five games in the 2023 MLS, they allowed a total of eight goals.
