Chicago Fire Showing Signs of Improvement

Ezra Hendrickson got fired from the head coach position at the Chicago Fire on May 8. Since then, the interim manager Frank Klopas has led the team in 10 MLS games, with his team winning four of those, losing three, and playing a draw on three occasions.

The fact is that Chicago’s form has improved under Klopas, particularly in home matches. With him in charge, the Fire has a 1-1-1 record at Soldier Field. His men defeated the Eastern Conference leaders St. Louis City 1-0, while also tying 3-3 with Atlanta United, the team with the most valuable roster in the MLS.

In their most recent game in Chicago, they lost to high-flying Columbus Crew, but it was a 2-1 loss. Plus, Columbus scored its winning goal in the third minute of stoppage time.

Nashville SC Needs Points Before the Schedule Gets Ugly

Nashville SC is second in the East with 38 points after playing 21 games. These guys are 11-5-5 with a 30-16 score difference. It’s almost certain we’ll see them in the postseason unless something really goes astray for Gary Smith and his boys.

They’ve got another three games to play before the Leagues Cup break, and two of those look really hard. After the game with Chicago, they take on the Philadelphia Union, followed by a game against FC Cincinnati. Those two are going to be very tough matches, which is why they will probably focus extra hard on winning points against Chicago.

They definitely have the manpower to get a win on Saturday, with players like Hany Mukhtar and Randall Leal in top form right now. What’s got to worry them ahead of their trip to Illinois is that they haven’t been great on the road lately. In fact, they’re without a win away from home in three games with two losses and one tie.
