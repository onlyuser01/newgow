The Michigan Gaming Control Board has announced that Soaring Eagle Gaming will become the state’s 15th online casino and sports betting operator. Soaring Eagle Gaming has partnered with GAN and met Michigan’s requirements and is live now.

Soaring Eagle Gaming Will Operate under Eagle Casino and Sports

Saginaw Chippewa tribe’s gaming arm will operate under the name Eagle Casino and Sports. Michigan’s law states that all players that want to enjoy the operator’s services need to be older than 21.

Henry Williams, Michigan Gaming Control Board’s executive director, stated that with Eagle Casino and Sports’ official authorization, the market now has 15 providers, and players of legal age (21) can register on any of these platforms.

He also added that all federal tribes in Michigan are now offering online sports betting and casino service. Williams concluded by congratulating the Saginaw Chippewa tribe.

Online betting and casino gaming were launched in Michigan on January 22, 2022. The law states that only commercial and tribal casinos can obtain licenses. The legalization of the online wagering industry proved to be a major success as the total sports betting handle for the year was almost $4 billion and December only was responsible for $500 million of that handle.

Recently, Michigan was also accepted into the Multi-State Internet Gaming Agreement. Thanks to this development, the state will share poker liquidity with Nevada, Delaware and New Jersey. Michigan’s entry into the Multi-State Internet Gaming Agreement opened the way for other states, one of which is Pennsylvania.

Even though Pennsylvania is one of the largest gambling sectors in the US and is a massive proponent of interactive wagering, it showed little interest in joining MSIGA.
