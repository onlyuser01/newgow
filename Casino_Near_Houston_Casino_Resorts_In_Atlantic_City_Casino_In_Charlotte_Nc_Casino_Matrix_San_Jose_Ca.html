ADA – a tribute to a 19th century countess that became one of the world's biggest cryptos

What do a Victorian noblewoman and a mathematician from Hawaii have in common? Well, in the world of crypto, they are both inspirations for one of the biggest altcoins out there.

Cardano is the brainchild of Hawaiian entrepreneur Charles Hoskinson, who began developing it as an alternative to Ethereum in 2015 through his company IOHK. As an ETH co-founder, he wanted to use his deep understanding of how the second-generation platform worked to form a third-generation alternative. In other words, one better-placed to embrace future technologies such as machine learning and AI.

He put this to the test in 2017, when he officially launched Cardano. It was a Proof-of-Stake network, seen as less risky than the Proof-of-Work method used by Bitcoin and ETH in that it makes it less advantageous for miners to attack the network. It's also thought to be more environmentally-friendly, but there's a lot of debate about this between experts.

Hoskinson and his team also made it a peer-reviewed platform in that key decisions are made by members after consulting evidence-based methods: a science-based strategy that set it apart from its rivals.

Note: The platform made small steps at first, with ADA steadily rising in value over the first few years of operation. In spring 2021, however, came a big announcement. As part of Cardano's mission to 'bring banking to the unbanked', it announced a huge project in Africa, aiming to develop Blockchain technology across the continent. Around the same time, ADA enjoyed a dramatic rise in price, doubling in value to an all-time high of $2.

Such projects back up Cardano's claims to be a global force and it's safe to say that it's now an established top ten crypto network. It's also the reason why more and more websites, including online casinos, are now accepting it as a payment method. The next section will explore the pros and cons of using it.

Oh, were you wondering where the countess comes in? This is from Hoskinson's deep love of mathematics, and Victorian mathematician Ada Lovelace is where ADA gets its name from.

The pros and cons of Cardano

Like with most cryptocurrencies, one of ADA's key strengths is its speed. Blockchain's decentralized nature means transactions can be processed almost instantly, without going through the red tape that fiat currencies often do. If you deposit ADA into an online casino, then you should expect to see your money in your account immediately, although network delays are not out of the question.

It should also be cheaper to make ADA deposits. Again, there is no central authority to charge a transaction fee, and casinos generally charge less than for traditional currency deposits.

It's also extremely secure. As mentioned, its proof-of-stake nature makes it tougher for hackers, and fraud is virtually impossible. The only problem is if you accidentally send it to the wrong place – then it could be very difficult to get your money back. You should always double- or triple-check the details before sending the funds.
