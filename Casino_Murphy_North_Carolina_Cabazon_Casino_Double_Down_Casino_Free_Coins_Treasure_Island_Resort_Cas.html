Teleingreso: Spain's popular code payment solution

Teleingreso is a registered trademark of Esgiro S.L., a Spanish company that was formed around 2010 to provide payment solutions in the European Union and Latin America.

With online shopping becoming increasingly popular, a gap in the market became clear. Many people who shopped online didn't feel comfortable sharing sensitive banking information with merchants they didn't know, especially after numerous stories of fraudulent activity. What's more, some consumers didn't have a bank account, so buying things online was impossible for them.

That's where solutions like Teleingreso came in. The concept was simple – customers shopped online as usual but, instead of entering their card details at the point of purchase, they received a nine-digit Tele-entry code instead. They would then take this to the nearest Teleingreso outlet, which could be an ATM or shop, although ATMs only accept card payments.

Today, thousands of Teleingreso outlets are located throughout Spain. Major banks Abanca and Cajamar provide access via their ATMs, while Disashop accepts the method in its stores where customers can pay with card or cash.

There are also several affiliated networks in several other countries, especially in Latin America, as this simple yet effective solution continues to be popular among customers.

Pros and cons of Teleingreso

Teleingreso's main selling point is its security, and it's easy to see why. You don't need to register or share any personal or banking data, you just get your code and pay at the outlet of your choice. You have full control of the entire process – a big plus point for people cautious about buying items online.

It's also extremely accessible for people who otherwise wouldn't have access to online payments – this may include elderly people or customers who choose not to have a bank account. It's extremely simple to use and there are no limits to the number of times you can buy something via Teleingreso.

Another positive is that the service is free to use. Teleingreso earn their money from merchants and don't require anything from the customer. This gives it an advantage over some digital payment methods that charge consumers for using the service.

However, it's not all good news. Teleingreso is a lot slower than many other online methods which offer instant payments. Getting a code, going to the nearest outlet and processing the order may take a few hours and will obviously mean you get access to your good or service later.

Also, while several affiliated payment methods are available in countries across the world, Teleingreso is only available in Spain, so you'll either have to live there or be travelling through there to use it.
