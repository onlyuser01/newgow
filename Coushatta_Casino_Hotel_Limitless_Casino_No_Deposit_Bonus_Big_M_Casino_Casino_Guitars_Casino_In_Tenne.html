Support for Military Students

The creation of PENN Entertainment Military Scholarship at Penn State Berks in Berks County where PENN Entertainment is headquartered will be endowed through a multi-year $50,000 commitment aimed at helping student veterans and active-duty military students achieve their educational goals.

Commenting on the announcement, PENN Entertainment’s senior vice president of Public Affairs and chairman of the ESG Committee, Eric Schippers, hailed the partnership with Penn State Berks for the scholarship that will further strengthen the company’s support for the military and its local community.

“PENN is passionate about honoring and empowering our nation’s heroes and this scholarship adds to our overall military recruitment and recognition efforts,” Schippers said.

The new scholarship at Penn State Berks, part of Pennsylvania State University, provided as part of the commitment to helping student veterans and active-duty military students achieve their educational goals in a supportive environment will be awarded for one academic year.

All veterans, active duty, reservists, or National Guard members that are currently enrolled at Penn State Berks can apply for the scholarship. Based on funds availability and the condition that the recipient continues to meet the eligibility criteria of demonstrated financial need, the scholarship could be renewed.
