81,000 Fans to Come to Borussia Dortmund’s Stadium

Die Westfalenstadion is going to be packed on Tuesday, with more than 81,000 fans coming to watch the Borussia Dortmund vs Manchester City. Most of them will be rooting for the home team, including the fans from the Yellow Wall, who are famous for the amazing support they provide to Dortmund’s players in big matches like this one.

This explains why Borussia Dortmund is very tough to beat at home. This team is without a loss in Dortmund since the summer, with three wins and two draws in the previous five home games.

One of those draws happened against Bayern Munich in Der Klassiker, in which Dortmund’s striker Anthony Modeste made it 2-2 in the 5th minute of the stoppage time.

Manchester City’s Main Focus is the EPL, Not the UCL

After securing a spot in the knockout stage of the UEFA Champions League, the Citizens don’t have to worry about this competition for a couple of months. Instead, they can focus on the Premier League, where they’re trailing behind Arsenal.

Because of this, their coach Pep Guardiola might decide to rest a couple of his key men in Tuesday’s game. One of those is Erling Haaland, the former player of Borussia Dortmund. With him on the bench (or playing for a brief period of time), the English team might not be as lethal as with him on the pitch. Still, this doesn’t mean that they’re not going to pose a serious threat to Dortmund’s goal.
