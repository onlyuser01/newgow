Live! Casino Pittsburgh Helps Prevent Local Tax Increases in Westmoreland County

Posted on: July 18, 2023, 01:03h.

Last updated on: July 18, 2023, 03:52h.

Live! Casino Pittsburgh in Greensburg, Pa., has provided significant benefits to Westmoreland County and its municipalities since the Pennsylvania Category 4 satellite property turned on its slot machines in November 2020.

The Vandergrift Casino Theater is among the more than 30 beneficiaries of local casino tax revenue generated by Live! Casino Pittsburgh in Westmoreland County. The Pennsylvania casino has generated almost $21 million in local tax benefits through the county’s Local Share Assessment program since the casino opened in November 2020. (Image: Casino.org)

Live! Casino Pittsburgh is operated by The Cordish Companies, a Baltimore-based gaming and hospitality firm. The company sent its executives to Pittsburgh this week to celebrate nearly $21 million in local tax benefits that the casino has delivered to 38 area municipalities and organizations in Westmoreland.

Category 4 “mini-casinos” were authorized in 2017 through Pennsylvania’s expanded gaming package. Each satellite property is permitted to house up to 750 slot machines. An initial allotment of up to 30 table games costs an additional $2.5 million fee, with 10 additional tables permitted after the casino’s first year of operating table games.

Live! Pittsburgh is a satellite casino tethered to Cordish’s full-scale casino property in Pennsylvania, Live! Casino Hotel Philadelphia. Live! Pittsburgh is one of four mini-casinos in the commonwealth, with the others being Hollywood Casino York, Hollywood Casino Morgantown, and Parx Shippensburg.

Satellite Leader

Of the four satellites in Pennsylvania, Live! Casino Pittsburgh has been the most successful to date. The 100,000-square-foot facility replaced a former Bon-Ton department store at the Westmoreland Mall.

In 2022, Live! Casino Pittsburgh generated gross gaming revenue (GGR) of more than $110.8 million. Hollywood York was a distant second among the satellites at approximately $90.9 million.

Under Cordish’s host agreement with Westmoreland, the Live! Pittsburgh casino shares 2% of its gross revenue with the county. Since November 2020, that local allocation, officially called a “local share assessment,” has delivered Westmoreland almost $21 million in new revenue.

The Westmoreland County government has used the new revenue stream to benefit various county services and nonprofit programs. The casino funds have helped purchase fire and police equipment, fund infrastructure improvements and public playground and park improvements, and covered stormwater upgrades. The casino money has also supported numerous charities, economic development arms, and government programs.

“We knew from the beginning that this was going to be a huge benefit for Westmoreland County, and I am glad to see it happening,” said Pennsylvania State Rep. George Dunbar (R-Westmoreland). “Otherwise, these projects, funded by Live! Casino revenue, would either be on the shoulders of taxpayers, or they wouldn’t happen at all.”

State Senate President Pro Tempore Kim Ward (R-Westmoreland) added that state lawmakers are frequently asked for government funding. The casino, she said, “has been just a huge extra pot of money to be able to help the region.”

Casino Benefits

Local share assessment money from Live! Casino Pittsburgh has gone to an array of projects.

A few notables include $322,990 to the Economic Growth Connection of Westmoreland, a nonprofit economic development corporation. That money will support volunteer firefighter companies, the Laurel Highlands Workforce and Opportunity Center, student athletics, and the Southwest Greensburg Borough.

Westmoreland County and its Westmoreland County Industrial Development Corporation received $751,475 to purchase new park equipment and help fund improvements to the New Kensington Advanced Manufacturing Park.

Greensburg City collected $700K for HVAC upgrades for the Palace Theatre and Stark Building, as well as to assist in renovating the Nevin Arena. Vandergrift Borough meanwhile received $447,443 for a dump truck, police safety cameras, and renovation of the Vandergrift Casino Theater and Vandergrift Library.
