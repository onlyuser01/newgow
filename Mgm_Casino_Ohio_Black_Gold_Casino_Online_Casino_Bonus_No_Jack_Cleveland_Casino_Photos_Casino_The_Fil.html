NJ Suspended Temporarily Betting on Ukrainian Table Tennis

Earlier this week, betting on Ukrainian table tennis in New Jersey was suspended. The temporary suspension was introduced by the New Jersey Division of Gaming Enforcement (DGE). Effective from July 9, all sportsbooks in the state were told to stop offering betting on all table tennis matches in Ukraine. Furthermore, sportsbooks in the state were instructed to stop offering betting on any matches at any location with the participation of six players. According to the regulator’s order, no betting can be offered for matches with the following players: Liliia Zaitseva, Anastasia Efimova, Eduard Panichev, Ivan Gaysin, Karen Dzhanibekyan and Gleb Zotov.

Earlier this week, the DGE was contacted by the Sports Wagering Integrity Monitoring Association. The association raised a concerns that the Ukrainian table tennis matches may be fixed. As a result of the suspected match-fixing, the ban on betting for those games was introduced. DGE statement on the subject said:

While there is not yet any evidence that any match fixing occurred in New Jersey, due the nature of the alert, the Division is suspending approval for wagering on all table tennis events in the Ukraine, including but not limited to the Setka Cup, WIN Cup, and TT Cup.“

Betting on Ukrainian Table Tennis Temporarily Banned in More States
