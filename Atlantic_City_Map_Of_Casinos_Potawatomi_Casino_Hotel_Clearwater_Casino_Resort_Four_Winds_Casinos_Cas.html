“Sportsbook and iGaming hosting is a very different landscape in the US compared to Europe that presents a number of challenges. This partnership allows us to get on with our core business, safe in the knowledge our technology stack is in good hands,” Noble explained.

The move marks an important step-up for Internet Vikings, which has been mostly focusing on iGaming businesses in Europe. With the PlayStar tie-up, the company will bring its expertise across the Atlantic once again, and in one of the pivotal gaming markets in the US.

The company already established its footprint in Colorado and West Virginia earlier this year with company CEO Peter Ekmark welcoming the new opportunity in New Jersey and the United States.

“We are extremely proud for two reasons. First of all, entering New Jersey is an achievement on its own. Currently, it’s the most sought after, yet difficult to enter, state in the US,” the chief executive said.

The company acknowledged the difficulties that are often associated with gaining market entry as the regulators expect a spotless performance by companies willing to set up their operations on the market.

Ekmark thanked PlayStar for putting their trust in Internet Vikings and said: “We are not planning to stop here. We will continue to expand and transform so that we can fulfill the needs of our clients at every step of their growth process.”
