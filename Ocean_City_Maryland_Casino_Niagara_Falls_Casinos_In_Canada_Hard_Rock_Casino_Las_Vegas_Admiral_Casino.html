All Eyes on the Miami Dolphins Offense

The Miami Dolphins are 7-3 in the 2023 NFL, scoring 30.5 points per game on average. That’s the highest average of all the teams in the league. However, their offense hasn’t been too productive lately, scoring just 34 points in total in their last two games.

Should this worry them ahead of the Dolphins vs. Jets Week 13 game on Friday? We think it should. The Jets are good defensively and the game is played on their turf.

Miami’s quarterback Tagovailoa is used to playing in sunny Florida, but in Week 13, he’s playing in New Jersey. The temperature in Miami is going to be around 80°F, while in East Rutherford, it’s forecasted to be somewhere in the ballpark of 45°F.

The good news, however, is that it’s not going to be a rainy day, nor will there be strong winds on Friday in NJ.

New York Jets Determined to End Their Losing Run

The New York Jets are 4-6 on the season, but three of their four losses happened in their last three games. Further, the last time they won, they did it against their NY rivals, the Giants, after overtime.

The reason behind their poor run is that they’ve been horrendous offensively. Quarterback Zach Wilson has been awful, which is why he got benched for Friday’s game. The Jets’ third-string QB Tim Boyle will take his place against the Dolphins, which probably won’t be much of an improvement.

Miami Dolphins vs. New York Jets Prediction

The Miami Dolphins are 10-point favorites against the New York Jets, which we think is just too much. Tagovailoa probably won’t throw for too many TDs at MetLife Stadium.

Plus, the Jets’ runners might do well on Friday. They probably won’t do well enough to win the game, but they can definitely help the Jets avoid a blowout loss.
