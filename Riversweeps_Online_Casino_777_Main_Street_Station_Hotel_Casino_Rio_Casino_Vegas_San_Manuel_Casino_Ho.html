Amaya - the poker specialist now part of one of the world's largest gaming entities

Amaya Gaming started life back in 2004 by creating land-based solutions for casinos and betting operators in Canada, but quickly transferred to the online betting industry with the growing popularity of internet gaming. The company became a leader in interactive casino, sports betting and lottery software, and acquired several prominent betting firms as their success grew.

The company hit the headlines in 2014 by announcing a $4.9 billion acquisition of Rational Group — the parent company of PokerStars and Full Tilt — making Amaya the largest public online gaming company in the world – it also placed them at the forefront of a hugely popular online poker market.

Yet less than two years later, Amaya suffered a huge controversy. CEO David Baazov was hit by five charges of insider trading from AMF, Quebec's securities regulator. The scandal rocked the company and Baazov, who had helped build Amaya into a gaming giant, was forced to quit the company.

Note: The controversy paved the way for the company to rebrand to The Stars Group in 2017 in an effort to distance itself from its former CEO. It also moved its headquarters from Montreal to Toronto, and traded under the initials TSG or TSGI — a nod to the success of its Stars franchises, including PokerStars.

Amaya (or TSG) has since gone from strength-to-strength in the gaming industry by expanding further into non-poker markets while retaining its position near the top of the online poker industry. In 2019, it announced a gigantic $11 billion merger with Flutter Entertainment that saw Flutter as the dominant partner. The new company boasted five divisions, housing major betting exchanges like Paddy Power, Betfair and SkyBet.

Today, players should feel safe using TSG content under the Flutter name. The company is a member of the UK's Betting and Gaming Council (BGC) which is dedicated to safe gambling and protecting consumers.
