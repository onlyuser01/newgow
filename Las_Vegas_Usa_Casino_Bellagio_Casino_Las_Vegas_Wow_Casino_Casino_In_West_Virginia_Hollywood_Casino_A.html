The leading multinational lottery operator, Allwyn, announced a new senior-level appointment. The company confirmed Friday it selected Naida Buljugic for the role of Global Chief People and Culture Officer. A statement released by the company reveals that the appointment is effective as of September 1, 2023, and marks the first such role.

For Buljugic, the appointment represents a promotion, as she has been a part of the management-level team at Allwyn'sCasinos Austria and Austrian lotteries for the last five years. During that time, she held the role of Head of HR. Now, as the company's first Global Chief People and Culture Officer, Buljugic will be in charge of Allwyn's People and Culture strategy across the globe. Moreover, she will play an important role in different talent development initiatives and oversee strategic organizational tasks.

Robert Chvátal, Allwyn's CEO, shared his excitement about the recent appointment and said that he is delighted to see Buljugic fill the new role. According to him, in her new role, Buljugic will spearhead growth and bring positive change across different markets. "She has demonstrated terrific leadership during her time at Casinos Austria and Austrian Lotteries, and in her previous roles at UniCredit and OSCE," added Chvátal.

He said that her extensive experience in combination with a strong understanding of Allwyn's culture makes her the perfect fit for the new role. Finally, Chvátal pointed out: "I look forward to working with her, as we continue to develop world-class talent across our markets for a world-leading lottery."

Sharing her excitement, Buljugic added that it is thrilling to hold the new senior-level role at such an important moment for Allwyn's growth. She highlighted the extensive experience that will help her in the new Global Chief People and Culture Officer role. "I have seen Allwyn grow and attract the best talent within the lottery and gaming sector, and I look forward to supporting that talent to thrive and develop at this exciting stage in Allwyn’s journey," said Buljugic in conclusion.
