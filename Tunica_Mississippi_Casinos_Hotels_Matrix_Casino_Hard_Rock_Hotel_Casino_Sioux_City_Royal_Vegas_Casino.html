BetCity.nl has become the latest Dutch betting operator to join the International Betting Integrity Association (IBIA), reflecting on the brand’s commitment to safer and responsible gambling. Presently, all Netherlands-based online betting operators must sign up with an integrity monitor body.

The BetEnt brand has picked IBIA, whose members account for EUR 115 billion ($137 billion) of annual betting turnover and almost 50% of all regulated commercial online betting activity worldwide. The association actively combats corruption and represents the gambling sector at high-level policy discussion forums such as the IOC, UN, Council of Europe, and the European Commission.

The move comes after IBIA announced, citing H2 Gambling Capital data, that in 2022 turnover for online sports betting is estimated to be around EUR 2 billion ($2.26 billion) and to double by 2025 to EUR 4 billion ($4.53 billion).

Protecting the Sporting Community through Transparency

BetCity.nl head of operations Kylian Olierook commented on the partnership, noting that BetCity is proud to become a member of the IBIA and combat corruption to protect the integrity of sports while also protecting patrons at the same time. Olierook went on to note BetCity’s commitment to protecting both sports and the consumers through transparency and its collaboration with the IBIA.

IBIA expanded to the North American market earlier in February. Chief executive Khalid Ali remarked that the association will play a critical role in supporting and protecting the growth of the betting industry in the US and Canada.

He went on to say that more member announcements should be expected in the coming months from various US states and Ontario. The regulator is active across six continents and members undergo due diligence processes and must commit to responsible betting practices.
