Malta Aims to Restore Its Reputation

Malta has recently employed increased scrutiny against operators, severely punishing offending companies. June saw the island nation’s Financial Intelligence Analysis Unit (FIAU) slap iGaming business group Glitnor Group with a $261,000 fine following several serious breaches. Shortly after, Tipster Limited lost its authorization to offer gambling operations under the MGA license, demonstrating the authority’s commitment to fair and responsible gambling.

The recent spike in high-profile punishments comes just before the upcoming European Commission investigation into Malta’s controversial Bill 55, aiming to provide legal protection to Malta-based iGaming companies from litigation in other European Union (EU) Member States. The MGA’s decisive actions likely aim to demonstrate the authority’s willingness to enforce its regulations and adhere to a higher standard as a regulator.

The Malta Regulator’s swift response in canceling Egmit Elite Limited’s authorization sends a potent message that unethical and illegal practices will not be tolerated in the Maltese gambling market.
