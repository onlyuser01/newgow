Cincinnati Bengals Dreaming Big in 2023

The Cincinnati Bengals feel as if the time has come for them to win their first-ever Super Bowl. Led by QB Joe Burrow – who could become the highest-paid player in the history of the NFL – they look capable of doing very well in 2023.

Obviously, Burrow is their most important player, but this season, the “cats” look strong in many other areas. In fact, one may argue that they do not have a weak spot this season. Of course, that remains to be seen when they go on the field on Sunday, but based solely on how their roster looks, they’re in for big stuff in 2023.

These guys did a great job strengthening their roster in the offseason, with the signing of Super Bowl LVII-winning offensive tackle Orlando Brown Jr. being their standout move. If he plays the way he did as a Kansas City Chiefs player, Burrow will get the much-needed protection, which in turn, will make him even more dangerous than before.

In the previous duel with the Browns, Burrow went 18-for-33 for 239 yards and two touchdowns, with one interception. With Brown Jr. as his bodyguard, he’s bound to do better this time. Or is he? Not if you’re asking Cleveland’s pass rushers.

Cleveland Browns Look Much Better than Last Year

The Browns’ strongest weapon for the past couple of seasons has been their pass rush. Myles Garrett is one of the best defensive ends in the league, the evidence being that he’s a four-time Pro Bowler.

In 2023, Garrett has a pass-rushing partner who could take Cleveland’s defense to a whole new level. The man in question is Za’Darius Smith who signed for the Browns in the offseason after a brilliant season with the Minnesota Vikings.

Apart from making their defense better, the Browns have also made a couple of nice acquisitions in the offensive team. Wide receivers Elijah Moore and Marquise Goodwin have arrived in Ohio, thus adding another option for the QB Deshaun Watson to aim his passes at. Of course, his No. 1 target is going to be Amari Cooper, who’s one of the best ball catchers in the NFL.

Still, Cleveland’s strongest offensive weapon is probably running back Nick Chubb who’s coming off a record-breaking season that saw him record 1,525 rushing yards – his career best.
