Barcrest – the old-school gambling company with a modern outlook

If you happened to visit a British pub in the 1980s or 1990s, the chances are, among the smell of stale beer and cigarette smoke in the bar, you'd have found a Barcrest fruit machine standing in a corner.

To many British people, the name goes together with famous beer brands like John Smith and Guinness, which is appropriate as the company that launched the Barcrest brand back in 1968 was Bass PLC, a famous UK brewer. Bass saw the potential for the fruit machines among pub regulars, who liked to play a game or two as they drank a pint. Their success led them other areas of the UK leisure industry, including arcade games and the ever-popular bingo, and later they expanded into the European market.

Fast forward 30 years or so later, and Barcrest were dominating the UK 'Amusement with Prize' (AWP) market, with 36,000 machines making up a 40% market share. It led to a multi-million-pound takeover by International Gaming Technology (IGT) in 1998, a deal which meant Barcrest content would be distributed globally for the first time.

As the new millennium approached, Barcrest adopted a focus on the digital gaming sphere. It launched a range of virtual slots, most notably Rainbow Riches in 2006, as a way of breaking into this new world of interactive world, and many online operators jumped on board.

The move was enough to attract the attention of gaming giant Scientific Games (SG), who bought Barcrest from IGT in 2011 and combined it with lottery operator 'The Global Draw' to form its new virtual gaming division.

Barcrest operates under the SG name today, benefitting from the latest game technology and continuing to release vibrant new slots games for a growing online market.

Barcrest slots

Most Barcrest slots come with a Big Bet option, that allows you to play for bigger prizes in return for a larger stake.

Rainbow Riches is its most famous game, inspired by the famous Irish legend of the leprechaun and the supposed luck that it entails. It operates on a simple 5-reel, 3-row format and you need to match three or more symbols on any payline to get a win. There are three bonus features – the 'Road to Riches', when you land three leprechaun symbols; the 'Wishing Well', where you choose one of three wells to get a prize, and the 'Pots of Gold', a multiplier that triggers when you land three pot symbols. There's also a free spins variant of this game, if you prefer.
