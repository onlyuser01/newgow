NextGen – over two decades of quality gaming content, now under the SGI name

NextGen (or Next Generation) Gaming began trading as a division of Next Generation Entertainment back in 1999. Its remit was to cater to the rising demand for online casino games in Australia, as well as to supply titles to popular land-based establishments.

The company slowly but surely gained a foothold in the domestic market over the following decade or so. By 2011, NextGen had an expansive selection of gaming titles, and a customer base to match, so it wasn't a surprise when another big-hitting provider offered them the chance to join forces.

Note: NextGen and Sweden-based NYX Gaming announced their merger in November 2011, creating a portfolio of over 300 games, and a global pool of clients. The partnership went on to new heights over the next few years and there was another major deal on the cards in late 2017, when Scientific Games (SG) appeared on the horizon.

The new alliance, worth $630 million, meant that NYX and NextGen were absorbed into SG's interactive division, known as SG Digital, or SGI. You'll find NextGen's output under the SGI banner today, as part of a giant gaming company that wants to be the leader in its field. New games still appear under the SGI/NextGen name, with the latter providing the mechanics behind each new title. Each game is subject to strict gaming regulations, including the respected Malta Gaming Authority license, so players should feel safe when playing on them.

Nextgen games and software

Nextgen's success has come down to its focus on catchy gameplay and impressive graphics which has earned it many admirers in the iGaming industry. NextGen titles come with SGI's state-of-the-art Open Gaming System (OGS) software, which allows partners to access thousands of games from many game providers.

Merlin's Millions is one such example of impressively animated aesthetics. Featuring Merlin himself standing at one side of the reels casting spells, the game spins along nicely, with its magic-related symbols and promises of features such as its Superbet – a kind of multiplier which can increase your win, but also increases your stake. Look out for the magic orbs too, three of which will give you free spins.

Nextgen also show off a range of animal-themed slots, ranging from Butterflies to Gorilla Go Wild. The latter has echoes of Donkey Kong, with its main character grabbing bananas every time you get a good spin. The temple symbols are the ones to keep an eye on though, as these will trigger the Gary the Gorilla Kingdom Feature, where you get to choose the bonus game you want.

If you're looking for games with a historical twist, then NextGen also have slots inspired by past events. 300 Shields is one of those, set in the times of the Ancient Greeks. Get three or more shields on the war-like reels to activate free spins – the more shields you have the more spins you get. There are also huge multipliers available, which help to make this title a popular choice.

Note: It can be a little tricky to adjust the bet amount and activate the auto spin. To do this, look for the settings symbol which should be next to the reels: once there, you can use the sliders to find your required levels.
