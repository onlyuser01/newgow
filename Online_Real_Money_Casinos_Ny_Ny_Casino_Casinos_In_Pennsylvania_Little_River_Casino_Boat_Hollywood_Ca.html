The British Columbia Lottery Corporation (BCLC) will expand its footprint in Western Canada through an agreement with the Saskatchewan Indian Gaming Authority (SIGA). The deal will see the former provide its proprietary PlayNow platform to the tribal operator.

BCLC Launches PlayNow through SIGA

The iGaming platform will launch in the Saskatchewan province at a later point in 2022. As per the agreement, it will be the one operating it will be the SIGA.

Thanks to the deal, Saskatchewan bettors will be able to access PlayNow’s varied offerings. The content will be fully localized and tailored to the local customers’ preferences. Therefore, players in the province will be able to play similar games to those they have grown to know and love. Additionally, punters will be able to engage in single-event sports betting – a betting format that became legal only a year ago.

The SIGA officially received the green light to provide online gaming options to the province last fall. Back then, the Federation of Sovereign Indigenous Nations inked an agreement with the province’s government that officially allowed it to offer iGaming in Saskatchewan.

Cavanaugh elaborated that this will allow local bettors to enjoy “the very best in online gambling entertainment.”
