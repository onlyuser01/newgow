  1. Do Not Play Airport Slots

Some players come and prefer to play on a budget. The reasoning is sound. If you have just a bit of money to spend on gambling, why not find a low-limit blackjack table and bet $5 or $10 at hand, as opposed to anything heftier than that?

Well, there is at least one reason we have found out. As it turns out, low-limit blackjack tables have a natural blackjack payout of 6:5 instead of 3:2. Evidently, you want to head for tables where the payout is 3:2 as it will guarantee a slightly better payout.

Now, from what we have seen in the years in Las Vegas, casinos do clearly mark the tables that pay at 6:5, which means it’s easy to spot those out and avoid them if you choose to. Some players who don’t mind taking on the extra risk and just enjoy the chase can still sit at a 6:5 table, but the payout is expected to be much poorer.

  1. Go for European or French Roulette

American Roulette is widely popular in Las Vegas, and this is no surprise. After all, it’s the homebrewed version of the popular French game. Yet, European and French Roulette tend to be so much more rewarding, and if you do happen to find these versions on the casino floor, please go ahead and stick with them.

The reason why lies in the simple fact that, unlike American roulette, these versions have only 37 pockets instead of 38 and a single zero, which reduces the house edge a little further! In fact, French Roulette may come with the En Prison or/and En Partage rule, which further offsets the house edge and gives you access to a few clever and rewarding mechanics.
