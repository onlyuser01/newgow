Manchester City Fighting a Multi-Pronged Battle

Pep Guardiola would love to see his boys win big in Leg 1 of the Champions League quarterfinal as it would allow them to switch their focus to other competitions.

The Premier League is what worries them the most, as that’s where they’re leading a two-horse race with Liverpool. Further, this team’s also in the competition for the FA Cup, which is where they’ve qualified for the semifinals.

Fighting on three different fronts isn’t easy for any team, even Manchester City, which is famous for its long bench. Guardiola has a large squad packed with amazing players whom he can rotate in order to get the best of them in every game.

Still, this doesn’t mean that Sky Blues can dominate every single match. This goes especially for the fact that they haven’t been great in the offense in the past few weeks. Sure, they did blow-out Southampton 4-1 in their most recent game, but before that, Man City played 0-0 in two straight games.

This could mean problems for them on Tuesday as they’re locking horns with a team famous for its defense.

Atleti Love Playing in England

On March 15, Atletico Madrid played at Old Trafford in Manchester against the Red Devils. Despite being the betting underdog, Atletico ended up winning 1-0. This wasn’t the first time Atleti beat a favorite in England.

Two years ago, Diego Simeone’s team did the same at Anfield. After playing 2-2 with Liverpool in 90 minutes, Atletico knocked the Reds out of the Champions League in extra time.

In both of those games, Atletico defenders did a wonderful job. Can they do it again? They might. You can be sure that Simeone’s strategy will be to “park the bus” in front of the goal, looking for chances from counterattacks. The only question is will this tactic work?
