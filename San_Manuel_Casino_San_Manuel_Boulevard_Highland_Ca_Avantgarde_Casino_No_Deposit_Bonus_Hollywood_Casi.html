The International Basketball Federation (FIBA) has revealed a new partnership with the Philippines-based sports betting operator, J9.com. As a result, J9.com will have access to all major basketball events hosted by the organization through 2024.

J9 will secure signage and presence in key FIBA competitions, including the FIBA Continental Cups for men and women as well as the FIBA Women’s Basketball World Cup 2022 and the FIBA’s main event, the FIBA Basketball World Cup 2023, and others.

This partnership marks the tenth official global partner for FIBA as the federation seeks to pivot its offering globally and engage with fans up close. FIBA is already in partnership with Bitci.com, a cryptocurrency trading platform, Chinese telecommunications giant Tencent, Wanda, and others.

J9 is hoping to significantly boost brand awareness for its own products and services. The operator will have access to the organization’s .basketball domain to promote various initiatives such as corporate social responsibilities through a dedicated portal, to name J9.basketball.

Commenting on this opportunity, FIBA director general of media and marketing services, Frank Leenders, said that he was excited to see J9 joining the FIBA family. He is confident that the collaboration will help advance basketball’s popularity globally as well as make sure that fans stay closer to the game through J9’s unique engagement options through betting.
