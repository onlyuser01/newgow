Bragg Gaming Group, an acclaimed and fast-expanding provider, supplier, and developer of iGaming content, has signed a new partnership with Rush Street Interactive, Inc. covering the regulated gaming market in Pennsylvania.

As a result, Bragg Gaming Group will deploy a range of its games and significantly expand its reach in the state, with a particular focus on its Spin Games content. Rush Street will see both its BetRivers.com and PlaysugarHouse.com gaming brands benefit from this new rollout with the content injected directly through Bragg’s new Remote Game Server technology.

The company is also using this opportunity to deploy brand-new content from its newest production arm, Bragg Studios, which will bring more worthwhile experiences to players in the Keystone State and the North American market in general.

Bragg has already used its RGS technology to implement its products in New Jersey and Michigan in the United States and Ontario, Canada. The company is also live in Mexico with its content through a tie-up with Caliente Interactive.

The expansion with Rush Street Interactive marks a significant milestone for the company’s own ambitions to achieve further geographical expansion and reach. Rush Street is already available in Latin America, Canada, and the United States, and this content partnership may serve as the stepping-stone of future launches.

In the meantime, Bragg Gaming is deploying a range of excellent titles, including Fairy Dust, a slot developed by its Atomic Slot Lab studio. The content that will be available at Rush Street moving forward in the Keystone State also includes games by the company’s Powered by Bragg aggregation and partner program.

Commenting on this new content rollout, Bragg Gaming Group CEO Yaniv Sherman said that the company was very happy to launch its in-house and third-party content with Rush Street Interactive and its brands.

"We are delighted to roll out our new RGS technology and content with a leading operator in a large and growing US market and look to continue our ongoing work with RSI as we provide their Pennsylvania players with engaging content and a great gaming entertainment experience," Sherman said.

The deal was welcomed by Rush Street Interactive CEO Richard Schwartz who said that the addition of Powered by Bragg and Bragg Studios constituted a tremendous success for the company and its iGaming brands in North American markets. He hailed Bragg’s content as the type of games that "clearly resonated" with BetRivers and PlaysugarHouse’s audiences.
