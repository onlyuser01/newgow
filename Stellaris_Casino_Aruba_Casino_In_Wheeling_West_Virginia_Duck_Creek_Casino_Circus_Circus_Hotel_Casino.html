Hawkins boasts 14 years in the gaming industry and will bring a wealth of experience to the team

Quick Custom Intelligence (QCI), a leading business intelligence solutions provider, has reinforced its team with the appointment of the experienced Alicia Hawkins as its new gaming business intelligence manager.

Hawkins boasts 14 years in the gaming industry and will bring a wealth of experience to the team. She excels in driving business improvement through data-driven strategies and will be a valuable asset to the QCI team.

Hawkins’ career started in tribal gaming where she specialized in slot operations analysis, contributing to revenue growth and enhanced customer experiences. Following that, she joined Boyd Corporate as a database management and data quality control officer. In that position, she helped the company make informed decisions based on reliable data.

Prior to joining QCI, Hawkins served Everi Holdings, further cultivating her gaming industry expertise. She worked together with a number of gaming partners, supplying them with data-driven solutions.

Boasting a Bachelor of Science in Data Science, Hawkins also boasts an impressive skill set that encompasses database management, slot operations analysis and data quality assurance. In addition, QCI’s new hire has impressive abilities in vendor relations and problem-solving.

CEO Thomas Welcomed Hawkins on Board

Hawkins commented on her new position, saying that she feels privileged to be able to join QCI’s team as its gaming business intelligence manager.

QCI beautifully bridges technology with business operations, and as customers gain a passion for data-driven decision making and leverage data for business optimization, I have the privilege of embarking on this journey with them. Alicia Hawkins, gaming business intelligence manager, QCI

She was welcomed on board by Ralph Thomas, QCI’s chief executive officer. The CEO said that his team is looking forward to seeing the impact the new appointee will have on the company. According to him, she will play an important role in the rollout of new product offerings.

Alicia will ensure data integrity and will lead in educating users on effective software utilization. Her hands-on experience will serve as the foundation for the guidance she provides, enabling our clients to make well-informed, data-driven decisions. Ralph Thomas, CEO, QCI

Thomas concluded that QCI is certain that Hawkins will be a valuable addition to the team. Her appointment highlights the company’s commitment to providing cutting-edge solutions, the CEO said.

Two months ago, QCI unveiled a partnership agreement with DigitalWin. Earlier this year, the company expanded in Australia with Gaming Dynamics.
