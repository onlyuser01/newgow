Sports data specialist IMG ARENA released a new announcement, sharing that it secured streaming rights for the top two soccer divisions in Norway. As a result, the company will be able to provide betting-oriented broadcasts from the two leagues, expanding its content portfolio.

IMG Expands Its Sports Portfolio

Under the agreement, IMG ARENA is now in possession of global betting streaming rights for the Eliteserien and OBOS-Ligaen divisions, excluding Norway. Per the deal, the sports data company will get access to a total of 485 matches across the two leagues.

IMG Arena noted that matches in Norway are usually played between April and November, with fixtures often taking place on Sunday evenings. Each of the two competitions features 16 teams. In addition, the Eliteserien provides the best-performing teams with a shot at the famous UEFA Champions League.

This agreement will greatly expand IMG ARENA’s European content, adding to its global soccer portfolio. The company is looking forward to boosting its weekly and year-round offerings, allowing it to provide its betting partners with more matches.

IMG Is Committed to Supplying Operators with the Best Products

Knut Kristvang, the chief executive officer of Fotball Media, commented on the agreement, sharing that he is very happy about the agreement with IMG ARENA. He noted that this multi-year agreement will allow Fotball Media to significantly grow the Eliteserien and OBOS-Ligaen divisions, helping them reach many soccer fans all over the world.

Meanwhile, Mark Wrigley, IMG ARENA’s senior director of rights, said that the agreement with the Norwegian soccer leagues expands IMG ARENA with more products of quality. He is excited by the fact that the company’s partnering operators will be able to engage their customers with more soccer content.
