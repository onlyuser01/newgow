English Speakers Have Significantly Higher Risk of Problem Gambling

Earlier this week, the platform announced the results of its first-ever annual report. Gambless revealed that it has collected and analyzed data for the last year and compiled the report which includes the responses of 3,500 tests completed by users of the mobile app. The whitepaper analyzed different language groups to determine if one group may be more likely to be impacted by problem gambling. Consequently, the report found that English speakers are 57% more likely to be impacted by problem gambling when compared to Italian speakers.

As it turns out, the Italian speakers were the group that had the lowest score in terms of problem gambling. On the other hand, Russian speakers scored 28% higher than Italians, meaning that they are more likely to experience problem gambling, according to the new report. The ban on gambling advertising that is active in Italy since 2019 is one of the reasons why that group had the lowest score. Additionally, the report said that English speakers may be impacted more because of “aggressive CRM marketing comms” by operators on the UK market.
