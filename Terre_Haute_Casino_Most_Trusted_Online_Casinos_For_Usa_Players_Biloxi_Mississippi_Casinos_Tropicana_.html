The new deal helps expand the collaboration between the studio and Rush Street, building upon a positive momentum gained between the two

White Hat Studios continues to expand the reach of its impressive content to new jurisdictions. On Wednesday, the company revealed that it boosted its partnership with the leading US iGaming operator, Rush Street Interactive (RSI). As a result, White Hat Studios delivered its content for RSI’s brands in two strategic US markets, Pennsylvania and New Jersey.

Thanks to the partnership extension between the duo, RSI’s brands PlaySugarHouse and BetRivers in the two states will deliver innovative content from the leading studio to its clients. This will help by expanding the offering of the two brands and seeing their customers engage with new and innovative titles from White Hat Studios.

At the same time, the collaboration further boosts the reach of the studio’s content, allowing it to engage with new audiences. The newly announced dual-state collaboration with RSI builds upon the positive momentum gained by the two companies.

Previously, White Hat Studios agreed to supply its content portfolio for RSI in Michigan, another important regulated gambling market in the US. Following an impressive debut in the state, the company’s games quickly gained popularity and became fan favorites.

The Deal Is Expected Benefit the Duo

Richard Schwartz, RSI’s CEO, shared his excitement about the deal acknowledging that it enables the company’s brands to provide new content. Moreover, he outlined that the rollout in Pennsylvania and New Jersey complements RSI’s growth strategy. Finally, Schwartz noted: “This follows the integration of White Hat Studios content in Michigan, which has since provided players in that state with amazing entertainment experiences.”

“Offering a wide selection of leading games is a key component of our growth strategy and partnering with White Hat Studios in New Jersey and Pennsylvania will deliver some of the country’s most popular slot titles to our players.“ Richard Schwartz , CEO at Rush Street Interactive

The new deal with White Hat comes after recently, RSI confirmed the launch of the BetRivers Slots Tournament in Michigan. The event, advertised as the “most exciting online slot tournament in Michigan history” grants online players a chance to win a share of $1 million in bonus money.

“We are pleased to be once again working with Rush Street Interactive in this latest capacity, having already collaborated to great success in Michigan.“ Andy Whitworth, CEO of White Hat Studios

Andy Whitworth, White Hat Studios’ CEO, was also excited about the collaboration with RSI. He said that the company is delighted to team up with Rush Street and at the same time acknowledged that their previous collaboration in Michigan brought success. Finally, Whitworth predicted that the deal in Pennsylvania and New Jersey will benefit the two companies.
