Information about fruit slots

Fruit slot reels are one of the most iconic images in the gambling world. The colorful symbols have tempted players to try their luck for well over a hundred years, going back to the famous Liberty Bell machine invented by Herbert Mills in 1907.

The Liberty Bell was a simple automatic mechanism with three reels that could read sequences to give a payout for certain combinations. Its popularity led to many copycat devices, including the Operator's Bell, which sometimes came with a chewing gum reward to circumvent strict gambling laws. The various flavours of the gum, the famous slot fruits, inspired the makers to use them as symbols and the fruit slot was born.

The machines stayed this way for over 50 years, until machine specialists Bally invented the first electromechanic fruit slot, Money Honey, in 1963 — a forefather of today's electronic games. The first video slot appeared in 1976, while Reel 'Em In became the first slot to offer a second screen bonus twenty years later.
