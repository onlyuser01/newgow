Las Vegas Icon Jimmy Buffett Dies at 76

Posted on: September 2, 2023, 10:34h.

Last updated on: September 4, 2023, 01:58h.

Jimmy Buffett, the singer-songwriter whose mellow tunes celebrated drinking margaritas and eating cheeseburgers on tropical beaches, died Friday night at age 76.

Jimmy Buffett performs his final concert in Las Vegas on March 11, 2023. (Image: the-sun.com)

“Jimmy passed away peacefully on the night of September 1st surrounded by his family, friends, music, and dogs,” a statement on his website said. “He lived his life like a song till the very last breath and will be missed beyond measure by so many.”

The statement didn’t cite a cause or place of death. However, sources told TMZ that Buffett suffered from skin cancer that turned into lymphoma. He had been in and out of the hospital for much of the last year of his life.

Buffett — who was born in Pascagoula, Miss. and raised in Mobile, Ala. — released more than 30 albums over his 53-year career. Most featured a unique blend of rock, country, and folk music set to the hypnotic beat of Calypso steel drums.

Though 17 of those albums achieved gold or platinum sales status, Buffett remained best known for a single song, the 1977 hit, “Margaritaville.” A paean to the beach-bum lifestyle, Buffett’s only Top 10 hit featured the universally recognizable chorus: “Wastin’ away again in Margaritaville.”

“Margaritaville” made Buffett a Baby Boomer icon, earning him a fiercely loyal legion of fans, called Parrotheads, who turned up in droves for his concerts wearing leis and loud Hawaiian shirts.

“There was no such place as Margaritaville,” the singer told the Arizona Republic in 2021. “It was a made-up place in my mind, basically made up about my experiences in Key West and having to leave Key West and go on the road to work and then come back and spend time by the beach.”

Multimillion Dollar Brand

“Margaritaville” was such a phenomenon, Buffett was able to parlay it into an empire of bars/restaurants. Following an unsuccessful first attempt in Gulf Shores, Ala., the second Margaritaville — which opened in Key West, Fla. in 1985 — took and led to franchises throughout Florida, New Orleans, and California.

For more than 20 years, Parrotheads have enjoyed margaritas and cheeseburgers in paradise at Jimmy Buffett’s restaurant and bar on the Las Vegas Strip. (Image: eventup.com)

The most famous Margaritaville opened at the Flamingo in Las Vegas in 2003. It boasts five bars, two patios overlooking the Strip, and a volcano that spills margaritas into two 300-gallon blenders.

Though the desert is a far cry from the tropical settings of Buffett’s songs, Margaritaville transformed Las Vegas into a hub for Parrotheads.

And though he never performed a proper Las Vegas residency, Buffett made sure that nearly every tour with his Coral Reefer backing band included a stop at the MGM Grand Garden Arena.

Casinoville

In 2011, Buffett expanded his Las Vegas restaurant and bar to add the first Margaritaville Casino in an adjacent space — though his casino empire was supposed to have launched years earlier with the Margaritaville Casino Resort in Biloxi, Miss.

In 2007, Harrah’s Entertainment (now Caesars Entertainment) announced plans to build that property for $700 million on the site of the former Grand Casino Biloxi and Casino Magic Biloxi. However, the Great Recession of 2008 had other plans. The riverfront project was eventually built without a hotel for $48 million, and opened by Buffett with Grand Casinos founder Tom Brosiq in 2012. (Buffett performed at its grand opening.)

Though Margaritaville Biloxi closed only two years after opening, the setback barely dented the Margaritaville brand. It now includes resorts, a cruise line, apparel, footwear, and food and alcohol.

In 2016, Buffett landed at No. 13 on Forbes’ list of America’s Richest Celebrities, with a net worth of $550 million.

Despite his financial success, Buffett never abandoned his first love. He continued touring until a few months before his death. A hospitalization last year forced the singer to postpone two MGM Grand shows in October 2022 to this March. The rescheduled show on March 11 became his final Las Vegas appearance.

Buffett leaves behind his wife since 1977, Jane Slagsvol, and three children: Savannah, Sarah, and Cameron.
