How online slots work

Online slots started as digital versions of slot machines you may have seen at land-based casinos or gaming halls. Since then, game developers have been coming up with creative themes, features, gameplay elements, and other characteristics that are making slots the most popular casino games on the internet today.

Traditional online slots are made up of reels with a range of symbols on them. You spin the reels and hope the symbols create a winning combination on an active win line. They may also come with special features, which are triggered either on random or by landing a certain number of special symbols. These most commonly include free spins or other types of bonus features.

Many modern online slots come with innovative gameplay elements, like cluster pays, in which winning combinations are created by clusters of symbols touching each other, as well as many other inventive features. With thousands of casino games created by hundreds of companies available, you can find pretty much anything.

Mathematics of online slots – house edge, RTP, and variance

Slot machines are configured to give the casino an advantage, also known as house edge. The average house edge of online slot machines is around 4%, which means that players lose 4% of the wagered amount on average in the long term. That said, the house edge can me lower or higher, depending on the specific slot machine and gambling website.

The RTP (return to player) – also known as payout ratio – is calculated as 100% minus the house edge and expresses the percentage of wagered funds players can expect to get back as individual wins, statistically. Games with a 4% house edge have an RTP of 96%.

Meanwhile, volatility (also known as variance) expresses how big and frequent individual wins are when playing a given slot machine. High volatility slots have a potential for large wins, but winning spins tend to be less frequent. On low volatility slots, you can win more frequently, but individual spins are usually smaller.
