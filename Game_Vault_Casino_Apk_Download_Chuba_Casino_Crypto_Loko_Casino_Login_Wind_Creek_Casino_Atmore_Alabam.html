Asian slot games leader Spadegaming joins iSoftBet’s list of partner operators for the latter’s Game Aggregation Platform (GAP) offer.

iSoftBet Announces New Agreement with Spadegaming

Asian-based online games provider Spadegaming will now be included in iSoftBet’s huge network of aggregated titles, which features over 8,000 titles from more than 70 game studios. Known as GAP, this solution offers a wide r range of games, making it possible to reach more potential customers, particularly in Asia. Spadegaming content will also benefit from bespoke iSportbet’s gaming tools like iNgame.

GAP enables operators to access new game content, was created to “maximize revenue potential by combining iSoftBet gaming content with that of over 50 leading content providers in the gaming industry.”

The platform is one of the most advanced within the sector and it offers a large selection of game genres, from slots to blackjack and roulette to bingo, video poker, mobile gaming and many more to some of the largest operators worldwide.

The platform already operates in 19 regulated markets including Belgium, Switzerland, Italy and Croatia.

GAP Further Expands Content Thanks to Spadegaming

Commenting on the partnership, Chief Commercial Officer at iSoftBet Michael Probert has said that he’s thrilled to welcome Spadegaming in the team and looks forward to keep expanding the offer “through the addition of stellar providers.”

He also said that Spadegaming’s content “is a true differentiator and will attract many more players to the offering.” With this deal, iSoftBet is confident GAP is going to “hit new heights.”

Dimitrios Malegkos, Sales Manager at Spadegaming, stated: “iSoftBet’s GAP platform houses one of the industry’s most expansive slot collections and we’re delighted to partner with them.”

For Malegkos, finding new ways to create engaging content is a must and is possible thanks to the agreement, as well as reaching more players than ever before.

iSoftBet won last month the Compliance Outstanding Achievement Award at the Vixio Global Regulatory Awards. This is a significant achievement for the company, just weeks after appointing Lars Kollind as new Head of Business Development.

The supplier’s compliance team has been at the leading edge of managing a series of changes this year in a bid to comply with regulatory changes in markets such as the UK, which is undergoing a thorough review of the Gambling Act, and obtaining certification in Sweden, Switzerland, Malta, Colombia, Bulgaria and Germany.
