Lotto Hessen will be among the first companies to try out the new terminals

Scientific Games announced that it has signed a new contract with Lotto Hessen in Wiesbaden, Germany. As agreed, the supplier will provide the lottery company with over 2,200 of its Contour.v terminals. In addition, Lotto Hessen’s gaming system will be converted to Scientific Games’ cutting-edge SYMPHONY system.

The Contour.v terminals are some of the best machines Scientific Games has to offer. The agreement will therefore help Lotto Hessen modernize its offering, allowing it to remain ahead of the curve. Boasting clerk and player HD screens, contactless player interactions and mobile scanning, the terminals will instantly set Lotto Hessen apart from its competitors.

Lotto Hessen will notably be one of the pioneers when it comes to the Contour.v terminals. As a result, the company will be among the first to benefit from the camera-based terminals’ features, transparent ticket scan area, HD screens and advanced optic technology.

The machines furthermore sport a sleek, uni-body that eliminates unnecessary parts, making the newest line of Contour terminals very durable and reliable. The terminals also offer easy processing and a variety of options for software upgrades.

The deal expands the existing partnership between Scientific Games and Lotto Hessen. The former company already provides Lotto Hessen’s gaming systems technology, instant game management system and sports betting solution. In addition, Scientific Games supplies the company with instant scratch games and the Scientific Games Enhanced Partnership program.

Lotto Hessen Is Glad to Deepen Its Partnership with Scientific Games

Lotto Hessen’s managing director. Martin J. Blach, noted that his team has worked with Scientific Games for over two decades. The lottery company has grown to trust the supplier thanks to its reliable products.

Commenting on the current deal, Blach explained that the addition of the new terminals is in line with Lotto Hessen’s modernization efforts.

The camera-based Contour.v terminals are part of our overall plan to continue innovating that experience and responsibly adapting to retail trends and consumer behavior. Martin J. Blach, MD, Lotto Hessen

Meanwhile, Matthias Müller, Scientific Games’ VP of sales and marketing, said that the launch of the new terminals was prompted by the supplier’s desire to innovate and respond to the needs of the increasingly complex lottery ecosystem.

The Contour.v terminals signify a new era for Scientific Games with the most advanced optics and pattern recognition software. Matthias Müller, VP of sales and marketing, Scientific Games
