888 Subsidiary Settles in Gibraltar

Earlier this week, an 888 subsidiary, Virtual Global Digital Services Ltd, settled with Gibraltar’s Gambling Commissioner for a sum total of £2.9m. The settlement concerned specific shortcomings that the subsidiary had comitted when dealing with customers, including Anti-money Laundering and Enhanced Due Diligence practices.

The Gambling Commissioner said that the subsidiary had offered full cooperation with the investigation and allocated additional staff to ensure that the matter is resolved expediently, which also played a factor in determining the final amount of the settlement.

Among the shortcomings outlined in the settlement are failures to conduct sufficient checks in the cases of specific customers whose source of wealth was not properly vetted. Licensing authorities such as Gibraltar and Curacao have been stepping up their efforts to strengthen the regulatory rules that they apply to locally registered gambling companies in a bid to ensure that companies comply with well-established industry standards.
