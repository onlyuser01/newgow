Regulators Face a Daunting Challenge

Regulating gambling in the modern era is no small job. It’s an emerging industry and a technologically complex one that poses a never-ending series of challenges to regulators – particularly to those whose oversight duties typically centre around things like land-based casinos or the lottery.

Online gambling is an entirely different animal. Just getting a digital gambling industry up and running is a feat by itself, and then the real challenges begin.

Rapid technological advancement: The rise of modern forms of gambling and betting creates new challenges for regulators seeking to ensure that these platforms are operating in a sustainable manner. This is particularly difficult in the digital realm, where the global nature of online gambling can make it challenging to enforce local regulations across borders. Lack of deep education: Regulators entering new territory are broadly unfamiliar with the nuances of the industry, largely by no fault of their own. The gambling industry is as complicated as any other industry in the world. But the learning curve can make it difficult to create well-conceived rules that keep pace with the latest developments. Limited resources: Many regulatory agencies are underfunded and understaffed, which creates practical problems when it comes to effective enforcement. This shortfall can create situations where operators are simply able to evade the oversight process. Balancing the needs of stakeholders: The interests of various groups of stakeholders can sometimes conflict with each other, making it challenging for regulators to strike a proper balance with their solutions. Whose advice do you listen to when you’re not certain what the best course of action is?

Lawmakers face some of these same challenges during their legislative process, and they often seek out input from the industry itself to help them craft a sensible law. Regulators, however, are the ones that are ultimately tasked with turning the words in the statute into tangible rules for operators and meaningful protections for customers that can stand the test of time.

These challenges are not insurmountable, but overcoming them requires a cooperative effort from regulators, operators, and other stakeholders. And it requires education.
