PaySera: a Lithuanian startup with many services, including online casino deposits.

PaySera began its journey in Vilnius, Lithuania back in 2004. This was a time of big growth in the online payments industry: PayPal had just replaced mailing checks and bank transfers as eBay's dominant payment method, proving that digital payments were now the best option for internet purchases.

The online revolution had begun. Startups all around the world took inspiration, and PaySera joined the party under its original name 'Elektroniniai verslo projektai': later shortened to EVP International. With just three employees, it launched an SMS payment service, now known as LightSMS.

In 2010, the company started its payment gateway PaySera Checkout, an innovation that would help them become Lithuania's first enterprise to earn an Electronic Money Institution (EMI) License (issued by the national bank) two years later.

In 2014, and with a 50-strong workforce, PaySera became the first Lithuanian company to offer QR payments to its customers. It now also had a fully functioning mobile app for users.

Since then, PaySera has continued to grow. It has over 300 employees based in several European countries, and Algeria. It's available on Google, Android and Samsung Pay, is part of the SEPA Instant Payments System, and even offers customers the chance to trade in gold.

As an EMI-licensed company, PaySera is duty-bound to provide a secure payment service, but it has its positives and negatives like any other provider.

Fintech payment methods like to stand out from traditional banks by offering instant payments at no extra charge. PaySera is one of these, so you should expect your funds to arrive at their intended destination right away.

Setting up your PaySera account is free, and you should also be able to make payments for nothing. This is another typical feature of Fintech providers.

Negatives

There are ATM withdrawal fees

Every time you withdraw money from an ATM using a PaySera card, you'll have to pay a fee. At the time of writing (February 2022), this is €1 per operation for a Euro withdrawal in an EU country, and can be as much as €1.50 plus a 1.8% currency conversion charge if you withdraw a non-Euro currency in the EU, or any money outside the EU.

PaySera's terms also make it clear that inactive accounts will be charged a monthly maintenance fee after lying dormant for two years. If you sign up, or are an active user, and this concerns you, we recommend you ask PaySera about this.
