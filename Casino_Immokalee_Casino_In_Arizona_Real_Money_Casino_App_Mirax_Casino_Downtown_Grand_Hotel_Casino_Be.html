GVC Holdings’ efforts towards safer gambling continued today as the company became the first gambling industry partner for the innovative responsible gaming tool PlayPause, which will be implemented within the BetMGM sports betting app.

Focus on Responsible Gaming

Developed by GeoComply, a leading supplier of geolocation compliance technology, and offered free-of-charge for gaming operators via a non-profit organization, Conscious Gaming, the tool is designed to modernize and strengthen the effectiveness of the industry’s responsible gaming programs.

PlayPause was launched in September, during the Responsible Gaming Education Week, adding new capabilities for operators to manage compliance requirements across multiple states. The solution expands the self-exclusion bubble for players allowing the protection to move along with them in cases they move from state to state.

“We are delighted to be the first official partner of PlayPause. We believe the most responsible operator will be the most successful operator, and PlayPause’s innovative, multi-state approach to responsible gaming in the U.S. aligns with our firm commitment to providing our customers with the safest and most enjoyable experience possible.” Shay Segev, CEO, GVC Holdings

The responsible gambling tool empowers operators to make more data driven decisions based on greater player insights so that if one player has self-excluded in one state can be recognized and protected in case they try to gamble in another. PlayPause also allows players to self-exclude from multiple states to prevent an incident gambling when crossing a state-line.

“A multi-state approach to responsible gaming is an important step for the industry and advances consumer protection in a meaningful way. We’re delighted to play a leading role in collaborating on this important and timely initiative.” Adam Greenblatt, CEO, BetMGM

Managing and operating separate self-exclusion databases for each state increases the burden on gambling operators, but PlayPause has successively tackled the issue by streamlining the administrative burden and eliminating the silos for each database.
