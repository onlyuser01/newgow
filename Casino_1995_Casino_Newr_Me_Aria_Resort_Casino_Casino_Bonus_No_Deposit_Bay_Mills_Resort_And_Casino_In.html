Max Verstappen Going for the Record on July 2

Max Verstappen is one of only three drivers who have won the Austrian GP on three occasions. The other two drivers – Jo Siffert and Alain Prost – did it a long time ago. In fact, the Dutchman is the only active driver with three Austrian GP triumphs, meaning that he’s the only one with a chance to get to break the record this Sunday.

That seems like great motivation for the Red Bull Racing driver to give his utmost on July 2. Another reason for that is that he wants to keep on impressing. Verstappen is sitting at the very top of the World Drivers’ Championship standings with 195 points after eight races. That’s 69 points more than second-placed Sergio Perez.

The most impressive fact about Verstappen’s 2023 campaign is that he finished on the podium each time this season, winning six races and finishing second in the other two.

Can Lewis Hamilton Upset the Odds?

Together with Michael Schumacher, Lewis Hamilton is the record-holder for the most Formula 1 titles with seven championships. The last time he won the F1 title was three years ago. Last season, he finished 6th, mainly because of car problems.

In 2023, he’s doing relatively okay. With 102 points, he’s currently sitting in the No. 4 position. Although he hasn’t won a race this season, he’s finished on the podium three times. Two of those podium finishes happened in the previous two events.
