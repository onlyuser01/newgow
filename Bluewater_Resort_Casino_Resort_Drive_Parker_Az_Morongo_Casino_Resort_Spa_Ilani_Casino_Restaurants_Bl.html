A Must-Win Game for Liverpool

There are several reasons why anything but a win over Bournemouth would be seen as huge upset for Liverpool. First of all, the Reds are desperate for points as they got only two after three Premier League rounds.

They played 2-2 with Fulham on the road, after which they drew 1-1 with Crystal Palace at home. Finally, on Monday, they lost to Manchester United 2-1. As a result, they’re now seven points behind table leaders Arsenal, and five points behind the #1 EPL title favorites, Manchester City.

That said, failure is not an option for them if they want to challenge for the trophy this season. Another reason why they’re expected to win is that they usually do great on their turf. At Anfield, Liverpool are unbeaten in the Premier League for more than a year.

They also have a fantastic home record against Bournemouth, with four wins in the five EPL games they two have played so far. An interesting fact is that in three of those, we saw three goals scored. We think something similar might happen on Saturday as well.

Bournemouth Praying for a Dignified Defeat

It’s very likely that everything in Bournemouth knows that their team is not going to do great on Saturday. The Cherries have won against Liverpool only once in the history of the club and we probably aren’t going to see them get their second victory anytime soon.

The thing is that they’re not a very good team. They played in the Championship last season and are among the top relegation candidates right now. Actually, the odds that they will get back to the Championship are just -250.

They did start the season in top gear, beating Aston Villa in the first round. However, in the following two games, they lost – Manchester City beat them 4-0, Arsenal won 3-0.
