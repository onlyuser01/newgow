Free Spins, Random Multipliers, and Prize Collections

As the much-anticipated country fair is fast approaching, everyone is busy getting ready for the rural celebrations. While engaged in the base game, players can walk on the thick grass, admiring the beautifully-designed fluttering butterflies, or using the miscellaneous rural tools there.

The wood-nailed standard play cards from 10 to A represent the low-paying symbols. The oven mitts, pieces of bacon hanging on a utensil, empty trays, meat cleavers, and knives and forks are not only the big-eyed hog’s worst nightmares but they also represent the high-paying symbols.

Players need to land between three and five identical symbols on adjacent reels from left to right to score wins. The game also has a wild symbol that can replace the rest of the symbols with the exception of the scatters and the Cash symbols.

Unexpectedly, players can also unlock a series of bonus features, such as the 10 Free Spins feature that requires a minimum of three scatters. During the same bonus round, players are also given a random multiplier ranging from x2, x5, x10, or x20 for the Free Spins round that is up next.

All wins collected during this bonus round, including Prize Collections, will, therefore, be multiplied, boosting the chances of landing some truly savory wins.

To engage the Prize Collection feature, plates need to land a minimum of three food symbols.

Once this occurs, a bonus lock and spin type of game is initiated. Players are handed three lives. Every time they fail to land any symbols on the reels during the round, players lose one life. When all lives are lost, players will claim their wins.

When a symbol lands during the Prize Collection round, its value is added to the pot, and all lives are replenished. The symbols will stay in their positions, thus cutting the number of slots where additional symbols can land.
