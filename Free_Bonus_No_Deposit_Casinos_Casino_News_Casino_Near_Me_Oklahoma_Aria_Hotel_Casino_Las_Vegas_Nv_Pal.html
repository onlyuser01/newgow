Miles Austin has become the latest high-profile sports official to be suspended from sports over alleged gambling offenses

The news has been confirmed by a source who spoke to ESPN, a media publication, and said that Austin had breached the NFL’s gambling policy.

Austin, who plays for the New York Jets, has denied wagering on NFL games through his lawyer, William P. Deni Jr, who spoke with various media representatives. Nevertheless, the wide receivers coach is said to be suspended from the league for at least one year, with the NFL denying any further comment until after the issue has been resolved.

Miles Austin Is Facing Investigation and Has Been Suspended

According to ESPN, the investigation against Austin has been going on for a while and it’s not either sudden or surprising to many NFL insiders. His lawyer, though, confirmed that Austin had participated in some form of gambling described as non-NFL professional sports and table games.

However, the NFL has a very strict non-gambling policy which means that players, coaches, and overall league personnel are all prohibited from placing a wager on any kind of sports from the big leagues or other organizations, including the NBA, NHL, PGA, MLS, MLB, NCAA, and so on.

This prohibition includes both international as well as local sports. The NFL has taken further issue with the coach, arguing that he had placed his bets while on the grounds of the team’s facility, which is also prohibited under current NFL rules. The NFL is confident that it has sufficient evidence to press on against the coach, having tracked his gambling activity to a specific website that ties him to the wagers.
