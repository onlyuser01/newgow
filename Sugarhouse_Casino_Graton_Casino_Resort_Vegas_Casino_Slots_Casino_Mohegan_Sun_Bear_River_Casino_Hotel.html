Oddin Joins Esic as an Anti-corruption Supporter

The Esports Integrity Commission (ESIC) announced today that the Czech esports live odds provider, Oddin, is now an anti-corruption supporter. By joining ESIC as an anti-corruption supporter, Oddin joins the battle against match-fixing and corruption within the esports betting space. Furthermore, Oddin would also assist ESIC in investigating suspicious betting activities with an ultimate goal to fight against cheating and betting fraud within the esports industry.

“We are very happy to become a member of ESIC.” Marek Suchar, head of partnerships, Oddin

The B2B provider of esports odds feed and risk management, Oddin, was founded in 2018. Since then, the company has been delivering the most engaging esports betting experience across multiple live markets.

Management of the Two Organizations Share Their Excitement

Marek Suchar, Oddin’s head of partnership shared his excitement by saying that the company is very happy to become an ESIC member. Furthermore, he acknowledged that the membership gives Oddin trustworthiness and durability within the online gaming industry. In conclusion, Suchar said that the company is looking forward to taking advantage of the benefits which the collaboration will bring.

“We are happy to welcome Oddin into our global anti-fraud network.” Ian Smith, ESIC commissioner

ESIC Commissioner, Ian Smith, shared his excitement by saying that ESIC is happy to welcome the company to its anti-fraud global network. Furthermore, he said that it is very important to have a key sports betting provider such as Oddin as a member. Another point which Smith raised is that with the help of Oddin, ESIC will fight against match-fixing practices in multiple jurisdictions. In conclusion, he said that ESIC is looking forward to having a long-term collaboration with Oddin.
