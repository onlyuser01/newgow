Aljamain Sterling to Shush His Critics

Aljamain Sterling is the champion of the UFC Bantamweight Division, but his reign’s been stained with controversies. Firstly, he won the title after Petr Yan got disqualified for an illegal knee. In his first title defense, he defeated Yan by a split decision, which many believed was wrong – including the UFC President Dana White.

Then, in his most recent fight, Aljo defended his title against T. J. Dillashaw. He beat him by TKO after Dillashaw dislocated his shoulder. For all these reasons, Sterling’s been under a lot of criticism, with some UFC pundits raising their doubts about whether he’s good enough to be a champ.

The good news for Aljo is that he can prove them wrong this coming Saturday. The bad news is that for that, he needs to defeat Henry Cejudo.

Henry Cejudo Coming Back in Style

Henry Cejudo is widely regarded as one of the finest fighters in history. He’s an Olympic gold medalist in freestyle wrestling, who used to hold two UFC titles before deciding to retire in 2020. Three years later, he’s coming back to fight again.

Henry Cejudo training really hard for UFC 288 pic.twitter.com/nFSFHppLy3 — Bloody Elbow (@BloodyElbow) April 28, 2023

During his MMA days, Cejudo won 16 fights, while losing only two. His first loss happened to legendary Demetrius Johnson, whom many consider to be the GOAT of the flyweight division. Cejudo’s second loss was a split-decision loss to Joseph Benavidez.

Since then, however, he’s undefeated with six wins in the UFC, one of which saw him exact his revenge against Johnson. What’s very interesting is that he won each of his last three fights by TKO, which is proof that this guy is an excellent striker in addition to being a fantastic wrestler.

Aljamain Sterling vs Henry Cejudo Prediction

Henry Cejudo is better at practically everything than Aljamain Sterling, with his only disadvantage being the fact that he hasn’t fought in three years. However, this doesn’t mean that he hasn’t been training during his hiatus. Actually, knowing how obsessed Cejudo is with fighting, you can be 100% sure that he’s spent a lot of time and energy plotting his comeback.

Pick: Henry Cejudo to win
