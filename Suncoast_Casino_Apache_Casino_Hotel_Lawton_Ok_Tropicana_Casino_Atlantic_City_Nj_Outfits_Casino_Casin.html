A short overview of GCash

GCash in its current form was launched in 2015 when smartphone technology was fueling rapid changes in the online payment industry. It was an updated version of an earlier payment system that had been around for over a decade which allowed users to pay for goods with their mobile phone bill or credit.

In 2015, paying for goods and services was becoming just as easy and efficient as paying through a desktop, and users were starting to expect companies to offer this capability.

Philippines-based Mynt aimed to meet this demand in Asia by launching an upgraded GCash service as one of its numerous Fintech solutions. The focus of GCash was to effectively turn a smartphone into a digital wallet, providing users with an app through which they could pay for a range of online items which were then charged to their phone bill or credit. The app was guarded by a special MPIN (mobile PIN), a four-digit passcode that authenticated all GCash transactions.

It has won several awards for its work, including Best Financial Inclusion Initiative, Outstanding Digital Finance Services, and Best Digital Campaign.

Today, GCash appears to be one of the leading digital wallets in Asia, consistently ranking as a top choice in the Philippines with a reported 20 million users.
