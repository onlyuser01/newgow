Today, we have put a list of gambling quotes that are both amusing and practical, and as is always the case with such things, they contain some truth about life itself.

Practical Gambling Quotes

The first round of gambling quotes we have for you have to do with the practical side of the game of chance. These sayings and phrases will serve as a good reminder that chance sometimes prevails and that what is important for you as a gambling man or woman is to be aware of the potential downsides of the activity.

“Few players recall big pots they have won, strange as it seems, but every player can remember with remarkable accuracy the outstanding tough beats of his career.” Jack King, Rounders

Rounders is one of the best movies about gambling and poker there is. In fact, it’s one of the reasons the late 1990s and early 2000s saw so much interest in poker. While the movie was not always truthful to the game of poker in practice, the dialogues were always deep and meaningful. As Jack King reminds us, it’s a good idea to remember the good days as well.

“Quit while you are ahead. All the best gamblers do.” Baltasar Gracián y Morales

Who would have known that a 17th-century philosopher will ever furnish the single most compelling and meaningful piece of advice any gambler may follow as a catechism. Gambling is doomed to end up costing, and this is why the more prescient observers, such as y Morales, recommend that you enjoy your luck and take it away with you.

“Play interests me very much,” said Hermann: “but I am not in the position to sacrifice the necessary in the hope of winning the superfluous.” Alexander Sergeevich Pushkin

Alexander Pushkin is known as a master of literature, and as is the case with every bright mind of our species, gambling is something that he touches upon. Pushkin dedicated his short story, “The Queen of Spades,” to one Herman who becomes obsessed with gambling, and in his passion, he becomes aware of a simple truth successfully captured in the quote.

“It’s hard to walk away from a winning streak, even harder to leave the table when you’re on a losing one.“ Cara Bertoia

On her cruise trips, Cara Bertoia had a lot of time to write, think, and naturally gamble. Her “A Novel About Casinos and Cruise Ships” has caught some of the hardships, highs, and lows of being a gambler, and she has instilled this wisdom in meaningful quotes such as the above. This Irish saying is the age-old revelation that chasing after your gambling losses can often have an unpalatable end that puts you in a difficult position. Sober up and remember that gambling is all about having fun!

“Gambling is not about how well you play the games, it’s really about how well you handle your money.” V.P. Happy

Even though Happy is a poker player, his piece of advice applies to pretty much anything we touch in a casino or at a sportsbook. It’s all about how you manage your bankroll, and that is why we believe this is one of the best gambling quotes you will hear today.

Gambling Sayings

Over the years, there have been many sayings and phrases about gambling. Some are offered as friendly advice to make sure you are aware of the dangers. Others tend to encourage gamblers to make the best decisions for themselves and enjoy a better yield. We have put together a mix of both.

“Scared money never wins”

This gambling saying contains much more meaning than you may guess at first glance. To make something out of your money, you will probably need to risk them first. Naturally, this is not always good advice when gambling, but it often leads to success in other areas of life.

“Good teams win, great teams cover.”

Since the point spread often comes with a better payout than an outright bet on the moneyline, many gamblers are genuinely interested in covering the spread, hence the saying. You don’t need a team to win or lose, as long as they cover the spread.

“A dollar won is better than a dollar earned.”

We all know the rush of winning money just like that. Whether this is real life or a game, the thrill of landing a win is always one that elates us. This is why the saying “a dollar won is better than a dollar earned” will resonate with anyone who has known the joy of turning a winning selection.

“At the gambling table, there are no fathers and sons.” Chinese proverb

The Chinese are wise people, and this proverb goes to show that one seated at a gambling table, there are no friends, just like a father and son would turn on each other, so would you and your friends.

“If you must play, decide upon three things at the start: the rules of the game, the stake, and the quitting time.” Chinese proverb

Another Chinese gambling saying has to do with how you approach gambling in general. In China, it’s wise to decide on what game you are going to gamble on, but also when you are going to quit and do so before you start playing.

“There are two great pleasures in gambling; that of winning and that of losing.” French proverb

Gamblers get endorphins released in their brains when they win and when they lose. The French, who invented a great many of today’s gambling games, have had a few centuries to figure this out before science, though.

“Better an ounce of luck than a pound of gold.” Yiddish proverb

Get lucky playing, and you will end up winning quite a bit more than a pound of gold. This is the logic that goes into this proverb. Of course, all high-risk reward games carry the danger of leaving you penniless, which is precisely what you should be on the lookout for.

“The best throw of the dice is to throw them away.” English proverb

Some societies disapprove of gambling altogether. This may not be the case in modern-day England, but it certainly was the case back in the day. That is how the proverb came to be, packing an ounce of wisdom in it and naturally proving an important guiding principle.
