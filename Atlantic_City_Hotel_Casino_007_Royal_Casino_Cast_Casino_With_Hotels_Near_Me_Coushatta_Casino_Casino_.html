Tom Brady Can’t Win Games on His Own (Or Can He?)

Tom Brady’s having another fantastic season with the Bucs, and many believe he’s going to explode in the postseason. After all, the GOAT always plays his best football in big games. The trouble is that in the MNF game, he’ll need his teammates to do great as well.

With Tom Brady as the quarterback and Mike Evans and Chris Godwin as wide receivers, Tampa’s passing game is bound to be brilliant. Their running game should also be top-notch, considering the form of running back Leonard Fournette. That said, the Bucs really shouldn’t worry too much about their offense. It’s their defense that should worry them.

Tampa’s defensive line will have to put pressure on Prescott to try and force him into another blunder. We think they’re very likely to do so unless the Cowboys switch their focus to running. In that case, the Buccaneers might be in trouble as their run defense isn’t great.

The Bucs defense gave up 120.7 rushing yards per game on average during the regular season, which made them 15th in the league. Plus, in their most recent game, we saw them concede 174.0 rushing yards – the fourth-worst performance of all NFL teams in Week 18. Still, to be fair to Todd Bowles’ men, the game practically had zero importance to them.
