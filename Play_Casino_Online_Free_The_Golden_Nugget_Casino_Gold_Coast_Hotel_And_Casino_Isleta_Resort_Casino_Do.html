Last Chance for Cincinnati Bengals

The Cincinnati Bengals are 5-6 on the season. They’re in the AFC North Division, along with the Pittsburgh Steelers, Cleveland Browns, and Baltimore Ravens. Winning the Division is practically impossible for them, but they still do have a chance to get to the playoffs via the wild card.

For that, however, they’ll need to start winning again. They’re on a three-game losing skid, but the biggest problem is that they’re without their starting quarterback. Joe Burrow, who’s the highest-paid player in the 2023 NFL, got injured two weeks ago and won’t return until 2024.

With backup QB Jake Browning, their chance of making the playoffs doesn’t look great. He went 19-for-26 in his first game as a starter, throwing for 227 yards, one touchdown, and getting one of his passes intercepted.

It’s not too bad given that the Bengals’ opponent was Pittsburgh, a team with incredible defenders. Nevertheless, Browning will have to do better this Monday if he wants to keep his team in the contest for a place in the postseason.

Jacksonville Jaguars are a Few Wins Away from Winning the AFC South

The Jacksonville Jaguars are 8-3 in the 2023 NFL season, which is two wins more than the Houston Texans and Indianapolis Colts, their rivals for the AFC South title. That said, the Jags are just a few wins away from securing the divisional title and stamping their ticket to the postseason.

Their schedule for the remainder of the 2023 regular season doesn’t look too difficult, but they will have a couple of tough games after the duel with the Bengals.

That’s why winning the MNF game is a must for these guys. Winning the game would give them a major confidence boost ahead of tougher matchups, as well as get them closer to securing a playoff spot.

They want to win on Monday night, but do they have the player quality to pull it off?
