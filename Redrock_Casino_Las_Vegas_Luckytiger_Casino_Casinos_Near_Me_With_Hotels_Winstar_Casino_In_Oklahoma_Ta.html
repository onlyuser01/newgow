Revolut – a Fintech unicorn that's now mainstream

London has a reputation for being a global hub for fintech start-ups, and Revolut is one of the many success stories from there. Founded by Nikolay Storonsky and Vlad Yatsenko in July 2015, it arose from a 'fintech incubator' in the Canary Wharf – a type of collaborative program that searched for new financial ideas.

Revolut stood out from other start-ups with the level of funding that it attracted. It earned 'unicorn' status after securing $250 million from DST Global in 2018 and it received a Challenger bank license in the same year.

In 2019, it started to offer commission-free stock trading to its customers who could invest on Nasdaq and the New York Stock Exchange via the Revolut app. Soon after it agreed a partnership with Visa that opened up 24 new markets and brought in 3,500 more employees.

Another huge funding round just before the pandemic put the Revolut's value at £4.2 billion, and, possibly fuelled by lockdown, its customer base expanded considerably.

After applying for licenses in the UK and the USA, Revolut started to enjoy mainstream success in 2021, with around 15.5 million customers in its database. Its status as the UK's most valuable Fintech start-up is confirmed, and we can now consider it as a true rival to established high street banks, such as Barclays and HSBC.
