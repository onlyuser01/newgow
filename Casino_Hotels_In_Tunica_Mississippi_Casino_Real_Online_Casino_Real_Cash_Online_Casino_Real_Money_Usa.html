UK gambling licenses and legislation

Gambling Commission is the only authority that regulates and licenses gambling providers in Great Britain. This includes national lottery, bookmakers, brick-and-mortar casinos, and, of course, online casinos.

The UK gambling commission was set up in 2005, under the Gambling Act of 2005, to regulate commercial gambling in Great Britain. Then, there has been the Gambling Act of 2014, which requires any operator wishing to interact with consumers in Great Britain to have a license from the Gambling Commission. This means that all online casinos which want to offer their services to players from the UK need to have a Gambling Commission license.

Gambling Commission aims to make sure that:

gambling is conducted in a fair way,

gambling isn’t a source of crime or disorder, associated with it, or used to support it,

vulnerable people and children are protected from harm or exploitation.

It’s clear from these objectives that player protection really is a priority for the Gambling Commission, which is why players from Great Britain (England, Scotland, and Wales) should always play in casinos that have a license issued by it. What’s more, casinos without a Gambling Commission license operate in Great Britain illegally and should be avoided.

Apart from the Gambling Commission, there are other regulatory authorities which might be of interest for players from the UK. The strict rules related to the Gambling Commission apply just to Great Britain. Gambling in Northern Ireland is regulated under the Betting, Gambling, Lotteries & Amusements (NI) Order 1985, which is quite old and doesn’t account for online gambling very well.

Additionally, prior to the Gambling Act 2014, subjects without a Gambling Commission license were allowed to operate in Great Britain. Licensed operators from so-called "whitelisted countries" (EEA countries, Alderney, Antigua and Barbuda, Gibraltar, Isle of Man, Tasmania) could provide their services to English, Scottish, and Welsh citizens, but this was revoked in 2014 and is no longer true.

Starting from April 14, 2020, the use of credit cards to gamble is banned in Great Britain.
