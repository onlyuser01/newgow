The US Supreme Court has put a possible end to the long-wounded saga involving the Seminole Tribe of Florida and pari-mutuel businesses that objected to a tribal gaming compact signed in 2021 that essentially allows tribal operators to launch online sports betting in the state.

The matter has been circulating in courts for the past three years with SCOTUS finally reaching a decision that the gaming compact was legal and could proceed. The only possible challenge to the compact at this point could be issued at the Supreme Court of Florida after the federal government has given the green light.

Pari-mutuel businesses in Florida argue that they stand to suffer economically if online sports betting is allowed. In the meantime, Finland has confirmed that it will break up the state gambling monopoly and will introduce a new license model where Veikkaus Ltd. will not be the only company to operate gambling products in the country anymore.

This is done after careful consideration of the implications. A survey submitted to the government indicated that consumers are spending increasing amounts and time on unregulated gambling websites, leading to an increase in gambling-related harm. To this end, the Finnish government has decided to act and change the licensing model to allow more competition in.

Veikkaus has appeared mostly optimistic about the change, although previously, the company said that it expects to see job cuts as a result of market liberalization. In the latest cybersecurity incident, the New York Gambling Commission (NYGC) has been targeted by an unknown hacking group.

The NYGC confirmed that a security event did take place and that its systems have been compromised to an extent. Several state properties are known to have experienced a disruption of service as well. The attack has had unclarified consequences and the regulator had not reported whether data has been leaked. This is one of the latest incidents against gambling businesses following attacks against Caesars Entertainment and MGM Resorts International.
