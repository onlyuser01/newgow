Zimpler: A fast-growing fintech startup with an emphasis on fairness

Zimpler was formed in Stockholm by two entrepreneurs in 2012. Originally marketed as a mobile invoice solution called 'PugglePay', the startup honed its service into an instant payment provider by 2016 and rebranded itself as 'Zimpler'.

Starting out in a small office, it quickly gained a reputation as a fast-growing fintech company, expanding into multiple markets across Europe with their Bank Payments product and Zimpler Go — a Know-Your-Customer payment solution for merchants.

The company bases its philosophy around the Swedish word 'schysstare', roughly translated as 'fairer', and their service has been very well-received: they won awards from the Deloitte Fast 50 two years in a row as a result.

Zimpler are currently supervised by Finansinspektionen, Sweden's financial supervisory authority, and enjoy a Licensed Payment Institution Status because of this.

The great thing about Zimpler is its super-simple setup. You give them your mobile number, they give you a verification code and, hey presto, you have an account with them.

The company bases its service on speed, a big thing in today's world where users tend to want things done in seconds. Note: Zimpler is not available on the USA market, which rules out a huge number of potential users. In Europe, the participating countries are the UK, France, Germany, Finland, and the firm's native Sweden.
