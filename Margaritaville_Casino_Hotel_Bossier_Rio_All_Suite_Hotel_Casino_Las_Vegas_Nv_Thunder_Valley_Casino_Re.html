UPI – an Indian collective aimed at making instant payments more accessible

In 2012, the Royal Bank of India released a four-year vision that aimed to build a safe, efficient, and accessible national payment system. It was motivated by the thriving cash black market across the country, and by the fact that around 145 million families had no banking facilities.

The RBI delivered on its plan. In 2016, the recently formed National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI) - managed by the bank – launched the United Payments Interface. The service focussed on simplicity: users only needed a unique identifier, known as a UPI ID, and the recipient's ID, to make a transaction. This was a far cry from traditional services, which put people off with their complicated procedures.

With users able to send money and make payments in a matter of seconds, UPI grew exponentially over the next few years. It had become the world's largest real-time payment provider by 2020, with over 25.5 billion transactions in just that one year.

Its success has led to other nations, including the United States, Singapore, and several EU countries, trying to emulate UPI with their own national systems.

With plans to achieve a customer base of 500 million people within the next few years, UPI shows no sign of slowing down. Revolutionary new technologies, such as a voice-based payment service, are in the pipeline and help to ensure that UPI remains a leader in its field.

Pros and cons of UPI casinos and payments

To give a balanced assessment of UPI's service, we're going to use this section to detail the positives and negatives you should expect once you sign up.

The first thing to highlight is that UPI is a real-time payment service, which means that it transfers funds instantly, without the recipient needing to wait for them to clear. This is particularly useful for casino players, who can start playing as soon as the funds leave their account if they wish.

UPI also boasts enhanced security measures. It uses a single-click 2-factor authentication system to verify customers, as well as QR codes for mobile payments. Users don't need to share any sensitive banking info, they use a unique personal ID code instead.

Customers can also expect a wide array of merchant options when using UPI. As mentioned, the service covers many different industries, including transport, leisure, banking, and online casinos, so you could feasibly get through a whole day of activities just by using UPI.

Despite these advantages, we should also point out that UPI is geographically restricted, so you can only use it if you have an Indian bank account. That said, registered users can still use the service outside of India.

Also, while we believe UPI is a mostly-free and trustworthy service, it's difficult to find exact information about fees on its website, or through asking their virtual agent. For this reason, we recommend reading the terms and conditions before registering with UPI, to avoid any unexpected charges.
