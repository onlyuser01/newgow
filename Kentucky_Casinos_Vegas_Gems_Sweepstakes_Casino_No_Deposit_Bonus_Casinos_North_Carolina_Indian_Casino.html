Nikita Krylov Keeps Rising Through the UFC Light Heavyweight Ranks

Krylov’s UFC story started in 2018 and since he’s 4-4 in this promotion. That might not seem impressive, but all four of his UFC losses happened to elite fighters – Jan Blachowicz, Glover Teixeira, Magomed Ankalaev, and Paul Craig.

The fighters he defeated in the UFC are Ovince Saint Preux, Johnny Walker, Alexander Gustafsson, and Volkan Oezdemir. For his Round 1 KO win over Gustafsson, Krylov received the Performance of the Night bonus from the UFC.

Ryan Spann Ready to Upset the Odds

Ryan Spann is seen as the underdog in Saturday’s fight, but we don’t think this guy should be underestimated. The American is 21-7 overall with a 7-2 record in the UFC. He didn’t fight against as strong opponents as Krylov, but he does have some great wins in his portfolio.

For instance, last May, Spann defeated Ion Cutelaba by submission, which was his 12th win by this method. This isn’t a surprise knowing that he’s got extensive experience with Brazilian jiu-jitsu. He also has six wins by KO/TKO, as well as three by decision.

Nikita Krylov vs Ryan Spann Prediction

Nikita Krylov is the favorite for a reason, but this isn’t going to be an easy fight for him. Sure, the guy has 93% finishes in his career, but we suspect it could be different this time. Instead of Krylov winning quickly, we expect to see a real battle in Vegas, with the fight winner probably being decided by the judges.
