King Kong Cash review

Theme and Storyline

King Kong has fascinated the world since Edgar Wallace invented the colossal ape back in 1933. This version of him puts a crown on his head and a hawaiian shirt on his back. The art style suggests that there might be more influence from the Nintendo owned gorilla Donkey Kong rather than the cinematic giant.

He’s certainly got a more chill look at the top of the Empire State Building than the beast who is ultimately killed by beauty and a squadron of biplanes. Though the biplanes still make an appearance.

There’s plenty of great apes in the realm of slots, but only one is King.

Graphics, Sounds and Animations

The graphics are one of the major draws of this slot. The character of Kong is a cheery character snoozing beside the reels, ready to jump up and award the player a feature. His animations are crisp 3D renderings.

The reels themselves are adorned with a range of interesting symbols. The animals all come with kooky outfits, while the ace to ten symbols are in bold colours that jump out as they spin.

The sound scape is a little more limited than you’d expect from a slot with this much visual flare. But it does a good enough job of generating atmosphere. And it does so without overdoing it and becoming invasive or annoying.

Gameplay

The base game is a five reel slot with three rows per reel and twenty win lines.

To spice things up, there are randomly triggered features that can affect any given spin. When the features are triggered, King Kong jumps up and activates the feature himself. These features include things like respins or additional wilds.

All 5 randomly triggered bonus features

There is a range of bonus features as well, but no gamble feature. Though with everything else in play, you probably won’t miss a simple game of double-or-nothing too much.

Wilds, Bonuses and Free Spins

The Wild symbol substitutes for all symbols except the Scatter symbol.

Three or more Scatter symbols anywhere on the reels trigger a Bonus. There are five possible bonuses that can be triggered. In order to decide which one the player is to be awarded they must spin the wheel.

The five possible bonus games on the wheel are:

Barrel Blast Bonus : in this picking game, the player selects barrels to reveal their contents. Each barrel contains an object that is associated with a particular win amount (a multiple of the player’s bet per spin). Once the player gets three of a kind, they are awarded that multiplier. Conclusion

With its fun character and top notch graphics, King Kong Cash makes an excellent first impression.

It’s main downside for us, however, is the low RTP (95.79%) which is a little below average, and the low to medium volatility. Either by itself could be countered by the other, but both together make the slot useless for anyone pursuing our slot strategies.

If RTP and volatility are less important to you, you may well find a lot to like in this slot.
