Western Union: the former telegraph company that's now a leading payment service

Today's Western Union is very different to when it was formed – but then, that was over 170 years ago. Back then, it was at the forefront of the new telegraph industry which was connecting the growing number of US states.

After a merger of the nation's six largest telegraph firms, Western Union made the first-ever transcontinental telegraph between the Eastern USA and California in 1861, a milestone in the formation of the modern United States.

Telegraphy started a long decline over the next century, however, forcing Western Union to seek new industries. After ventures into the telex and telecommunications sectors, a full-scaled debt restructure and a takeover by First Data, it settled on the financial services industry in 2006. This formed the basis of the company that we know today, with a focus on wire transfers and mobile payments.

It proved to be a smart move. Booming sales allowed the company to acquire several financial service companies for a combined total of well over $1 billion between 2009 and 2011. One of them, Finint S.r.l., opened up over 10,000 subagents in Europe to the company, significantly expanding its customer base.

In 2015, Western Union launched its Connect service, signaling a move into app payments. WeChat and Viber were the two beneficiaries of the deal. It has since been hit with allegations of pushing smaller banks out of the market by the EU's financial regulator, however.

Western Union enjoys a status as one of the leading payment methods in the world today, serving over 150 million customers in 130 currencies, according to its own figures.
