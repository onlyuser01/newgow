AC Milan Wants to Keep the Good Run Going

AC Milan is without a single loss in all competitions in the last five games. During this period, we saw the black-and-reds score 7 goals while conceding just one. Before that, however, they got demolished by Inter 5-1.

After the loss in the Derby Della Madonnina, Stefano Pioli’s men have been fantastic at the back. Their offense could’ve been more productive but given that their recent schedule has been pretty tough, it’s fair to say that they’ve done okay.

Another explanation for why AC Milan hasn’t been doing as well offensively as some fans might expect is that a couple of their forwards only joined the club a few months ago – Christian Pulisic, Noah Okafor, and Luka Jovic.

Juventus Doing Great Despite Problems On and Off the Pitch

French superstar Paul Pogba is suspended for using doping. His Italian colleague Nicolo Fagioli is suspended for illegal gambling. These two midfielders won’t return to action anytime soon, but that’s not the only problem Juve is facing right now.

Juve will also be without two Brazilian full-backs, Alex Sandro and Danilo, who are injured. Speaking of injuries, forward Federico Chiesa will also miss the AC Milan vs. Juventus game on Sunday.

Sure, Juventus has plenty of backups to cover for them, but without those guys, it’s going to be difficult to beat AC Milan. In fact, the black-and-whites are going to struggle even to win a single point from their trip to San Siro.

AC Milan vs. Juventus Prediction

Pioli’s boys are undefeated against this opponent in the last five AC Milan vs. Juventus games. Further, the last time they welcomed them at San Siro, they won 2-0. We suspect something similar might happen this time as well.
