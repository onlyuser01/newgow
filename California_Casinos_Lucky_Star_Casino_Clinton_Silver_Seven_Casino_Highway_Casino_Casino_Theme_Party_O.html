Cazino Cosmos review

Theme and Storyline

The Cazino Cosmos is a strange steampunk, space opera world of clockwork animals dressed like Victorian Industrialists and humanoid cats in space helmets. Even more so than most of Yggdrasil Gaming’s output, this one leans heavily into a somewhat obscure world that clearly has its own internal rules but is not quite letting on what they are.

The setting within this world appears to be a space casino floating above a vast purple planet which fills much of the background, while in the foreground, on the reels, the oddball selection of gamblers play cards, sip brandy and flaunt magnificent Teutonic moustaches.

It is a cool place to play your slot games.

Graphics, Sounds and Animations

The level of graphics is a little plainer than some Yggdrasil Gaming slots, which are notable for being visually extravagant. The backgrounds are impressive, but on the whole, the reel and symbol animations are fairly simple.

Not that simple is bad. In fact, Cazino Cosmos manages to ring some of the most luxurious feeling out of a relatively standard set of tools. The soundtrack is high quality, the animations clean, and the general atmosphere created is decidedly Mississippi steam boat – but in space.

Conclusion

A world unto itself, this sequel to Cazino Zeppelin takes the next logical step and transports its weird aesthetic into space. The fact that this aesthetic works, coupled with the attractive volatility and reasonable RTP, makes this game a strong recommendation from us.

The only major let down is the rather uninspired gameplay in the main game, which is nicely countered by the range of bonuses.
