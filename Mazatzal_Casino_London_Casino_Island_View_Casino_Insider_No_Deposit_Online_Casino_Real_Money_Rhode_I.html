Chelsea Hoping to Keep the Good Run Going

After a disappointing couple of games last month, the Blues from London are playing great soccer in October. They’re 3-0 this month, with two of those wins happening in the 2023-24 English Premier League. Unfortunately for Chelsea’s fans, there’s a caveat – both of those wins happened against much weaker sides (Fulham and Burnley).

Still, this doesn’t necessarily mean that the Blues can’t keep the good run going. They have a solid roster, which cost the club owner hundreds of millions of dollars to assemble. There are some really talented players on the team, but many of them have yet to start showing their worth.

Nicolas Jackson, for example, who arrived from Villarreal for a fee of more than $40 million, has scored only two Premier League goals so far. His colleague from the attacking department Mykhailo Mudryk also needs to step up, as he’s got only one EPL goal to his name this season.

The bad news for them is that scoring against Arsenal won’t be easy as the Gunners are in top shape at the moment.

Arsenal Going for the No. 1 Place in the Prem

The Gunners are currently second in the Premier League with 20 points, which is the same as table leader Tottenham. The two also have the same score differential (+10), but the Spurs are higher because they’ve scored two goals more than Arsenal.

Mikel Arteta’s men have scored 16 goals thus far while conceding just six. This is really impressive given that they’ve played against some really tough opponents, including Manchester United, Tottenham, and Manchester City. All this has probably done wonders for their confidence, which surely is a big plus heading into the game with Chelsea.

Another thing going in the Gunners’ favor is the fact that Bukayo Saka and a few others might return after missing the previous game(s) due to injury problems.
