Albert Duraev is a Sambo Expert with Decent Boxing Skills

Albert Duraev has a background in combat sambo, which explains why nine of his 16 career wins happened by submission. He’s also a decent boxer, although he’s been using those skills almost exclusively for defending. The evidence of this is that he’s been landing 2.94 significant strikes per minute since joining the UFC while absorbing 2.53.

Duraev’s strongest weapon is takedowns. He seems capable of taking down almost any opponent, while he’s also pretty decent when it comes to defending against takedowns. In every single one of his four UFC/Dana White’s Contender Series fights, we saw him land at least one takedown. This is really impressive as all of his opponents were fighters with decent TD defending skills – Chidi Njokuani, Joaquin Buckley, Roman Kopylov, and even Caio Bittencourt.

Jun Yong Park Hoping to Break into Top 15 in the Middleweight Division

“The Iron Turtle” is 16-5 overall, with a 6-2 record since joining the UFC in 2019. He won his two previous fights by submission, and if he continues to finish his opponents, we might see him get ranked. But can he beat Duraev is the big question.

He’s got a similar set of skills to his UFC Fight Night 224 opponent. He’s great wrestling-wise, with decent boxing skills. The Korean lands a lot of strikes, but he also gets hit a lot of times. Same as Duraev, he too excels when it comes to takedowns. Park lands 2.33 TDs per fight on average, while his takedown defense is 70%.
