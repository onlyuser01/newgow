Philadelphia Union Looking Great in the Offense

Philadelphia Union played 2-2 with Orlando City earlier this week in a game which Daniel Gazdag skipped due to international duties. He played for Hungary against Lithuania on Wednesday, but is expected to return for the Union this weekend.

Philly’s playmaker is one of the best midfielders in the league. He’s a real commander in the center of the pitch, who’s got an eye for finding holes in the opposition defense. Gazdag and his teammates are great when it comes to game management, keeping the ball in their possession in order to stop their opponents from creating too many goal chances.

When it comes to scoring, that’s one of Gazdag’s roles, but with strikers like Julian Carranza and Mikael Uhre in front of him, he really doesn’t have the need to take shots too often. It’s just that these two are among the most complete forwards in the MLS – they’re fast, strong, great in the air, and incredibly lethal with their finishes.

Experience teaches us that the best time to witness their brilliance is when Philly is playing at home. Subaru Park is the place where Philadelphia Union has scored 17 goals in the nine MLS games this season while allowing just five.

Can Inter Miami’s New Coach Record His 1st Win This Saturday?

Inter Miami is 5-0-12 in the 2023 season. The club owner David Beckham and his partners blamed Phil Neville for this, which is why they sacked him from the position of manager a few weeks ago. They put Argentine coach Javier Morales in charge, but he hasn’t done much since the appointment.

On his debut, Morales saw his boys lose to Wayne Rooney’s DC united 2-1 at home. In his second game at the Inter Miami helm, his team got hammered by Bryce Arena’s New England Revolution 3-1.

Now, it’s time for his third game as Inter’s manager, and it seems the same thing is going to happen again. The thing is that his players just can’t get on target. At least not enough times. That’s strange because this team does have a couple of decent strikers on the roster. However, they’re probably only shine once Lionel Messi arrives.
