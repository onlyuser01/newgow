Avento MT Limited Receives Access to GiG’s Marketing Compliance Screening Tool

Leading iGaming technology company, Gaming Innovation Group Inc. (GiG), announced today that it inked an agreement with Avento MT Limited. Under the agreement, the Malta-based operator will receive access to GiG’s automated marketing compliance screening tool, GiG Comply.

The compliance monitoring tool allows its users to conduct automated compliance checks. Furthermore, the GiG Comply tool is easy to use and focuses on consistency. The compliance monitoring tool also enables the creation of checks for various regulations and requirements across all jurisdictions on any given website.

In a statement, Christine Bonaci, Senior Legal and Compliance Officer at Avento MT Limited commented that the company provides safety for its customers while at the same time follows all regulations. According to her, this has been a priority for Avento and with the help of GiG Comply, the company will be able to ease its work process.

GiG Continues to Grow

GiG’s Chief Commercial Officer, Ben Clemes, said in a statement that the company is very happy to sign Avento to the marketing compliance tool GiG Comply. Clemes added that GiG is proud to team up with a client like Avento Group that stays at the forefront of responsible gaming.

Undoubtedly, with this move, GiG continues on its path to growth and success. Back in November, the company revealed that it has successfully secured a vendor registration license with the Michigan Gaming Control Board. The license is applicable for the company’s affiliate website World Sports Network (WSN.com), allowing WSN to operate in the state.

Furthermore, on November 20, GiG signed an extension of an existing partnership with tourism and hospitality company KAK DOO Skopje (KAK). Under the partnership extension, GiG will provide retail and online sports betting solutions which even cover tailor-made omnichannel sportsbook. With that in mind, under the agreement, 25 North Macedonia sports betting venues are included.
