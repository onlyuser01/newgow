Amex: one of the most valuable financial brands in the world

Amex is one of the most recognized financial brands in the world, boasting millions of customers and offices on every continent. But the company didn't actually start off in the finance industry.

Three American entrepreneurs, Messrs Fargo, Wells and Butterfield, merged their businesses to create an express mail company in 1850, hence the name American Express. Enjoying a virtual monopoly in that field, they expanded into financial services by launching a money order business seven years later; issuing money orders — a kind of old-fashioned prepaid card — on behalf of their customers.

Amex forged an international presence around the turn of the 20th century through its revolutionary traveler's cheques. These allowed Americans to withdraw cash abroad and were even used by military personnel during the First World War.

The 1950s saw Amex become the first major issuer to introduce a plastic card, a predecessor to today's credit cards. These allowed customers to purchase goods on credit, with the amount to be paid back with interest.

The company embraced the digital age in 1995, forming ExpressNet, an early variant of today's website. Card members could check their accounts online, pay their bills electronically and even enroll in special rewards programs.

Today, a huge number of users around the world use Amex online every day, with over a hundred million active credit cards. The company is one of the biggest issuers around and will likely continue to be so for years to come.

Amex users can buy pretty much anything they want with their card, and it's easy to withdraw money in a foreign country. What's more, there is a great rewards system in place, so customers often enjoy discounts on shopping and travel via a points-based system.

Note: It's extremely easy to make an Amex online payment: the Amex app, available on iOS and Android, allows clients to make payments in just a few taps on a keyboard or phone screen. This is a far cry from early internet payments and gives the customer a lot of freedom.

That said, Amex's ease of use may not always be such a great thing. The very nature of credit cards - providing goods or services up front that can be paid for later - can lead users into a false sense of security. Spending electronic money is very different to handing over cash: we can easily lose track of how much we spend, not to mention the interest that can accumulate on top of it. Users should always exercise caution when using credit cards like Amex online, especially when trying to gamble safely.

In light of these problems, several national governments have banned the use of credit cards on gambling sites, such as online casinos. American and British users, for example, may have difficulty using Amex to make casino deposits, so this is also worth bearing in mind.
