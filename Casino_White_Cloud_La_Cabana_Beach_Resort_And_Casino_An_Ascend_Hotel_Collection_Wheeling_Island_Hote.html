Betsson Group has strengthened its content offering and portfolio across several jurisdictions with the help of Swintt, a trusted and established iGaming developer of innovative content. The two will see their newly-fangled partnership allow them to inject content directly to iGaming markets in Sweden, Germany, Malta, and Latvia.

Apart from Swintt’s original content provided through the SwinttGames and SwinttPremium portfolios, the company will also leverage its SwinttStudios platform to provide games by third parties that will add further variety to Betsson Group brands and what players in the aforementioned markets can expect to discover.

Betsson Group and Swintt will enjoy a very strong presence in Europe, and more rollouts are due to get underway before long. Swintt has confirmed that it will be expanding further in iGaming markets in Lithuania and Italy as part of its collaboration with Betsson. The games that players will experience feature numerous and well-established options by the company, including Aloha Spirit XtraLock, Book of Shai, Electric Elements, Duolitos Garden, and more.

Partner studios will also benefit significantly from this partnership as companies that are part of SwinttStudios will be able to bring some other excellent games, such as Treasure Capsule: Thor’s Strike by Samurai Studio. Another popular option that will be added is Stacking Bison by Fine Edge Gaming.

Swintt Chief Executive Officer David Mann was very happy to see this partnership take place and allow the companies to come closer together. This content boost will be an important part of Swintt’s strategy as the company continues to grow its regional footprint and add new opportunities for its brands and players.

"By working closely with Betsson, we’ll be able to ensure customers in a number of important markets get to experience the huge variety of titles that we have available from our SwinttGames and SwinttPremium line-ups, as well as exciting third-party games courtesy of the SwinttStudios platform," Mann further elaborated.

Betsson Group Head of Gaming Operations Sarah Micallef was similarly pleased with the chance to see her Group continue to grow its product offering to players in prominent markets such as Scandinavia and most of Europe.

The key to sustained success, Micallef argued was to ensure that a company has access to the best software providers. Over the years, Swintt has proven to be just that, she concluded.

Swintt recently teamed up with another prominent group, namely PressEnter, which is also a respected and multi-jurisdictional-spanning company.
