MGA Games, a provider of slot games, has further expanded its footprint in Portugal following its deal with bwin. Thanks to a new integration with SkillOnNet, the former comapny’s content will now be available with the famous international operator BacanaPlay.pt.

The casino operator is known as one of the best iGaming platforms for Portuguese-speaking audiences. It is adored by fans because of the best-in-class content it secures for its audiences.

BacanaPlay.pt will now be able to further diversify its games catalog by adding localized content from MGA Games. Some of the most notable products to be covered by the deal are MGA Games’ titles featuring various Portuguese Celebrities.

The supplier’s celebrity portfolio includes games such as Beatriz Moniz Estaca Prateada, Joana Araujo Capita da Sorte, Joana Albuquerque Planeta das Gemas, Sofia Sousa Deusa Jade, Maria Nunes Misterio no Circo and Teresa Guillherme Rainha da Persia.

The deal also covers other slot games such as Mina de Ouro Plus, Feiticeiro do Fogo Megaways, Campeao do Boxe Megaways and Sandro Rey.

Finally, BacanaPlay.pt will benefit from the Triple View design of MGA Games’ products, improving mobile play. As a result, customers will get to enjoy seamless gaming experiences with no interruptions and quick loading speeds.

Executives Praised the Deal

Javier Lanfranchi, MGA Games’ sales director, addressed the new agreement, saying that his team is excited to continue working with SkillOnNet and power BacanaPlay with games. Lanfranchi noted that his company has so far enjoyed a fruitful partnership with SkillOnNet, having previously leveraged the supplier’s platform to launch games with the Spanish brand PlayUZU.

We are convinced that this association will consolidate the presence of both companies in European markets. Javier Lanfranchi, sales director, MGA Games

Furthermore, MGA Games is thrilled to expand its presence in Portugal and reach more local players.

They [MGA Games] have shown us already in Spain how strong their games can be and we believe they will perform similarly well just west of the border. Jani Kontturi, head of games, SkillOnNet
