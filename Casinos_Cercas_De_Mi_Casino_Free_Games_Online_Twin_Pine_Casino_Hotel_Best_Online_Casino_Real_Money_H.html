Vegas Golden Knights Plotting Revenge for 2020 Loss

They say revenge is a dish best served cold, and three years seems long enough of a wait for the Vegas Golden Knights to get their payback. It was 2020 when they lost the Western Conference Finals to the Dallas Stars, and now, they seem more than ready to get ahold of the Campbell Bowl. If they do it, it’s going to be the second conference title in the history of the franchise.

The first time the Golden Knights won the West was in 2018, just a year after the foundation of the club. However, they failed to win the Stanley Cup – the Washington Capitals defeated them 4-1 in the Finals.

This year, Vegas boys are determined to get ahold of the holy grail of hockey. Their eyes are already set on the 2023 Stanley Cup, but to get there, they need to deal with the Dallas Stars first. Luckily, they seem to have the manpower to cruise past the boys from Texas.

The Golden Knights have an incredible roster this season, with Jack Eichel being their standout player. He’s been brilliant throughout the season, but it was the start of the playoffs when he really switched up a gear. He’s already scored six goals and recorded eight assists since the start of the postseason (11 games).

Jonathan Marchessault has also been great, along with several other Vegas forwards. The Golden Knights have also been decent in the defense, but there are some worries ahead of Game 1 of the Western Finals. Two of their goalies – Logan Thompson and Laurent Brossoit – are struggling with fitness and are likely to miss Friday’s match. Can the Dallas Stars take advantage of this?

Dallas Stars Love Playing in Vegas

The Dallas Stars won each of their three duels with the Vegas Golden Knights in the 2022-23 NHL season. Their first game of the season took place in Vegas in January and the visitors won 4-0. The two met again a month later, and the Stars won again.

This time, however, it was a much tougher game – the Dallas boys won after a shootout. Their most recent game went to penalties as well, and the Stars won once again.
