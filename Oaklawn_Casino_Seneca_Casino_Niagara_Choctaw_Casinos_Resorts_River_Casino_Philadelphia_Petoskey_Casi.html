The Canadian Gaming Summit is going to feature a range of experiences for industry specialists, with the "Leaders" conference track confirmed last month and now, the event revealing the arrival of the "Sports Betting" conference track that will add to the value of the gathering due to take place in Toronto, Canada on June 13-15, 2023.

The Sports Betting conference track has been set for Wednesday, June 14 at the Metro Toronto Convention Center where the event will be held. The conference track will focus on providing attendees with access to a variety of panels that tackle and discuss matters that happen exclusively in the sports betting vertical.

This includes but isn’t limited to discussions about regulations, integrity, player protection, leveraging social media content, and more to build a robust brand identity and equity. The conference track will open with the "Sports betting across Canada: navigating the patchwork of a provincial rollout" panel, which will speak about the regulatory changes that happened in the Canadian market since the regulation of single-sports betting in the country. Amongst the panellists are:

Doug Hood , Project Director Gaming Modernization, AGCO

, Project Director Gaming Modernization, AGCO Bruce Caughill , Managing Director Canada, Rush Street Interactive

, Managing Director Canada, Rush Street Interactive Mike Maodus, Associate, Blake, Cassels & Graydon LLP

The panel will be moderated by Salim Adatia, who is VP at Gaming Labs International. This is not the only anticipated panel, as there is also the "Partner and play – collaboration between Canadian teams, leagues and sportsbooks" discussion which will touch on how sportsbooks and sports organizations are collaborating. The panel will feature experts such as:

Aubrey Levy , SVP Content & Marketing, TheScore

, SVP Content & Marketing, TheScore Kuljeet Sindhar , Associate VP at International Gaming & Data Ventures for the NBA

The final panel of the conference will wrap up with another interesting and ground-breaking topic. The panel, "Esports betting in Canada: investing, reducing friction and opening up a new audience," will take a look at how sportsbooks and betting companies can engage with esports fans who have not been "tapped into" just yet. The panel will feature a number of specialists and experts in the field, including:
