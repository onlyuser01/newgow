Manchester City Mustn’t Stop Winning

Pep Guardiola’s team is without a single loss in more than three months. In fact, the Cityzens are 16-3-0 in all competitions since losing 1-0 to Tottenham on February 5. They’re also on an 8-win run in the Premier League, which has moved them all the way to the top of the table.

Even though only a few weeks ago they had a 15-point deficit behind Arsenal, they’re now above the Gunners. They got one point more than Arsenal, as well as one game fewer. What this means is that if they beat Leeds United on Saturday, they’re going to increase the point advantage to four points.

In simple terms, it would mean making one giant leap toward securing the EPL title, which in turn, would give them the freedom to focus on the holy grail of club soccer – the Champions League.

Some say Sam Allardyce is the epitome of an English soccer manager. In the past, his teams were always decent at the back, with all 11 players on the pitch working hard to protect the net. Knowing this, it seems that he’s the right man for the Leeds United job.
