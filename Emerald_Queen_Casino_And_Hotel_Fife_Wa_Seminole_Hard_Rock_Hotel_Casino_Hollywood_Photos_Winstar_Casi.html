The new edition of the Australian gambling education conference, Regulating the Game, will take place in London this September, attracting a wide range of participants

This September will mark the first time Regulating the Game (RTG), an Australian gambling regulation education conference will be held in London, the UK. As announced by IAG, the event will be taking place between September 25 and September 29, 2023. For five days, attendees within the gambling vertical will brainstorm ideas for the sector.

RTG London 2023 speakers are expected to include Cyprus Gaming and Casino Supervision Commission’s chief executive officer, Harris Tsangarides, the Massachusetts Gaming Commission’s chair, Cathy Judd-Stein, as well as ANJ’s chairwoman, Isabelle Falque-Pierrotin. Additionally, Leanne Oxley, UK Gambling Commission’s director of enforcement, the founder of Epic Risk Management, Paul Buck, as well as the gambling commissioner and executive director for Gibraltar, Andrew Lyman, are also among the speakers that have confirmed attendance.

As a part of the conference, a unique Pitch! networking event will take place, featuring innovative gambling companies and experts within the gambling vertical. This event will provide a unique opportunity for solutions and service providers to showcase products that stand out with innovation and safeguard the interests of stakeholders, including consumers. Armalytix, a data intelligence company that delivers solutions for gambling operators has secured attendance for the Pitch! the networking event, a recent report released by GamblingInsider suggests.

An Important Event for the Gambling Vertical

According to the organizer of the event, Senet, the five-day conference helps bring together international and Australian regulators. Those organizations, bringing extensive knowledge within their respective industries engage in discussions that seek to develop best practices within gambling regulation.

Without any doubt, the RGT conference will be one of the most sought-after and anticipated events for the gambling vertical this autumn. The event’s founder, Paul Newson, explained that the program is “designed to grapple with public policy challenges, cultivate curiosity and critical thinking, contest ideas, share insights and motivate an appetite for continuous improvement and regulatory and sector leadership.”

“We think it’s important that all stakeholders are engaged and active in the public square, contributing knowledge and insights, and hearing diverse perspectives to best inform public policy thinking and discussions, and help influence robust regulatory settings and approaches.“ Paul Newson, founder of Regulating the Game

He explained that the program will deliver content from experts and allow stakeholders to engage in debate regarding different opportunities and risks within the vertical. Existing issues as well as possible improvements to key regulatory policies will be discussed. Finally, Newson predicted that the event will seek to help develop an effective and innovative regulatory approach that is backed by evidence as well as research.
