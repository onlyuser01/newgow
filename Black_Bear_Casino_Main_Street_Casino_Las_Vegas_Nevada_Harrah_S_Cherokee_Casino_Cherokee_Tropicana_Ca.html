Jai Alai Was on the Verge of Disappearing in the USA

Jai alai is a sport that got nearly forgotten in the USA but is now making an unexpected resurgence. It is one of the oldest sports in the world, created in the 1800s, and plays like a high-speed combination of racquetball and padel. Players bounce a ball around a walled-in space by catching and throwing it at speeds of up to 130 mp with the help of a hand-held wicker scoop called a Cesta.

By the start of 2022, Jai alai’s only active professional scene was at Miami’s Magic City Casino. The venue served as a hub for athletes from the US, Mexico, France, Spain, and the Philippines. Despite being a niche sport, it offers a twist to help separate it from other leagues. In the USA, everybody can own a Jai alai team, with the price to sponsor one for a season starting at $100,000.

In October 2021, gambling operator BetRivers noticed the sport’s appeal and became the league’s official sponsor. Players across seven states can now regularly bet on Jai alai matches, watch live streams and pick their favorites from colorful teams like the Wall Warriors, the Cesta Cyclones, and the Chula Chargers. The sport is making a stunningly quick resurgence thanks to the enthusiasts’ continued efforts and support from big brands. The official Jai alai league has amassed over 130,000 followers on TikTok.
