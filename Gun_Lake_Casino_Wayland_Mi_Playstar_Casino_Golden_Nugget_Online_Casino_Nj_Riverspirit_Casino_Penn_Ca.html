Information about deposit bonuses in Sweden

In 2018, the Swedish online gaming industry received a boost. The Swedish Gambling Act broke away from the nation's previously conservative betting industry and welcomed private investment for the first time. This meant that foreign operators could offer services to Swedish customers, alongside domestic firms such as state-owned Svenska Spel.

While Svenska Spel benefit from their past monopoly and still control over half of the country's gambling industry today, the new rules mean there is more competition than ever between online casinos for Swedish customers. These sites often organize special promotions including bonuses for new customers, and the deposit, or welcome bonus is perhaps the most popular of these.

This is normally extra credit that is added to the player's account after they make a deposit. The casino in question normally adds this automatically, but players might need to enter a special code or send a short request email to activate it.

During the Covid pandemic, the Swedish government raised concern about the bonuses and their potential to encourage problem gambling. It announced an online bonus limit of SEK 100, or just under 10 euros, so players in Sweden have a more restricted selection to choose from nowadays.

Despite this, they can still be a nice way to get to know a casino and get a bit of extra money to play with, subject to certain conditions. However, with the changes in law, Swedish casinos can only offer one bonus per player at the time of writing. If that's the deposit bonus, then this will almost certainly just be the welcome bonus — the amount you receive after you make your first deposit.
