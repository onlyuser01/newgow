The provider of innovative technology for the land-based sector has teamed up with the tribal operator to further develop its business footprint and strengthen the operator’s casino gaming offering

As a result, Table Trac Inc and Blackfeet Nation have signed an additional contract that builds upon their existing more-than-decade-long relationship. Table Trac is a leading company that focuses on the provision of casino information and management systems, as well as the automated monitoring of key performances for partner properties.

Bringing Table Trac’s Solutions to Long-Time Partner’s Newest Location

The renewed partnership will see Table Trac Inc install the CasinoTrac CMS system at The Peak Restaurant in Montana, which runs as a satellite location to the Glacier Peaks Hotel & Casino through the appropriate licensure. This collaboration was welcomed by Table Trac Inc president Chad Hoehne:

It’s always an exciting and significant event when an existing customer chooses CasinoTrac to grow alongside them. Table Trac Inc president Chad Hoehne

Meanwhile, Glacier Peaks Casino general manager Heather Iron Shirt said that CasinoTrac would help consolidate the experience at The Peak Restaurant and deliver a great product to customers. The two companies go back a very long way indeed, with their first collaboration established in 2011 and bearing fruit over the past decade.

“Most important, is being a partner with a community leader of the Blackfeet Nation’s stature. Their recent collaboration driving human trafficking awareness and active shooter training is something to be proud of, real-time issues of universal concern,” Hoehne stated.

The Blackfeet Indian Reservation hosts 17,321 tribal members and the tribal operator leverages its sovereignty and established footprint in the gaming industry to maintain financial stability for its members as well as address other tribal matters.

Table Trac Remains Committed to Growth and Strengthing Partnerships

Table Trac has been working to actively promote itself as the go-to solution to land-based properties seeking to consolidate their operations and boost core performance results. The company has recently gone on a hiring spree, bringing in Theresa Birtalan as head of new business, Thomas Florio as business development in Eastern US and new territories, and not least – Jeff Baldi as SVP of commercial strategy.

Table Trac has been maintaining healthy relationships and collaborations with both the commercial and tribal gaming sectors, and the company intends to continue doing so in the future. The company similarly has multiple ongoing collaborations that have been growing over the past ten and more years exemplifying Table Trac’s ability to deliver consistently robust results for its partners.
