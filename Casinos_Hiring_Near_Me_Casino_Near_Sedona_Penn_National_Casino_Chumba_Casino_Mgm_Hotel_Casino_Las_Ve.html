About Paysafe and Paysafecard

Paysafecard itself traces its roots back to Vienna, Austria, where it was set up by a small group of entrepreneurs. It still has its headquarters there, but has since expanded to over 50 countries in the world, spanning five continents.

In that time, it has been subject to a pair of acquisitions. First, Skrill, which can also be used in Skrill casinos, took them over in 2013, and then UK-based Optimal Payments Group absorbed the Skrill empire two years later. Optimal Payments liked the name of the card so much that they later rebranded the company to Paysafe Group, as it's known today.

The company, including Paysafecard, is regulated by the UK Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) and boasts a fruitful partnership with the world-famous Mastercard. In March 2020, the companies announced an expanded relationship, integrating Apple Pay and Google Pay into the Paysafe payment system.

What is Paysafecard?

Paysafecard is a payment method based on vouchers that range in value and contain a 16-digit code, which can be used to pay for products and services online.

The vouchers can be purchased at a wide variety of physical locations, such as grocery stores, gas stations, newsstands, etc. The company has a network of partners in individual countries, so the exact stores you can purchase Paysafecard vouchers will depend on your country. Use the Paysafecard store finder to find the one closest to you.

Paysafecard Mastercard and account

In general, you do not need to create a Paysafecard account to pay for goods and services online – the only thing you need is the 16-digit PIN from the purchased voucher. However, there is an option to create a My Paysafecard account, which allows you to make larger transactions, keep track of your vouchers, and also make casino withdrawals using this method. Without this account, you cannot withdraw funds using Paysafecard.

Also, there is an option to request a Paysafecard Mastercard, which is linked to your My Paysafecard account. You can use this card just as you would use any other Mastercard debit card. Just be aware of the associated fees, which are charged for ATM cash withdrawals, adding money to your Mastercard, etc.
