Land-based gambling in Nigeria

When it comes to gambling, Nigerian law tries to reduce illicit activities such as money laundering, which the country has had problems with in the past. For this reason, it prohibits many types of betting activities, such as roulette, dice games and non-skilled card games.

That said, lotteries, land-based casinos and sports bookmakers are permitted, but only those with a license. The legal casinos tend to be located in the biggest cities, such as The Federal Palace Hotel in Lagos, and difficulties in obtaining a license often lead to the operation of illegal casinos across the country.

Note: The National Lottery Regulatory Commission is responsible for regulating gambling activity and can issue heavy fines or three-month prison sentences for people and entities who break the law, such as for betting under the legal age of 18.

Despite the strict law, gambling is popular among Nigerians, with a recent Cambridge University study showing that around 20% of the population gamble every day. Unfortunately, this also means that problem gambling statistics are a concern, with the same research finding that 57.2% of school children have gambled at least once in their lives.

Whether this is down to poor regulation or law enforcement is a topic up for debate, but it's also true that many Nigerians have a healthy attitude to gambling, and the activity remains a popular social pastime.

Online gambling in Nigeria

Nigerian law doesn't mention online gambling in its statutes, meaning there are no restrictions on which sites citizens are permitted to play on, so it's perfectly legal to play on both domestic and foreign online casinos. The result is that many people prefer to bet online instead of going to their local betting shop, as there is less risk involved.

However, like with land-based gambling, Nigerian operators must have a license from the National Lottery Commission to offer online games to customers, and these can be difficult to obtain. The penalties for those entities caught breaking the law are equally as severe as for operating illicit land-based services.

Players can choose from a wide range of betting operators, particularly those based abroad.
