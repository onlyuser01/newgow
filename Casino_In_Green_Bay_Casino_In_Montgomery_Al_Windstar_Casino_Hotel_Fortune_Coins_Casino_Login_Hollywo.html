What to Expect from Teixeira vs Blachowicz

Jan Blachowicz has been one of the most prominent MMA fighters. The Polish national has forged his legacy back at home and the professional Ultimate Fighting Championship over the past years with his track record now at 28-8-0.

He has posted a six-fight winning streak and in the past fights. He managed to secure wins against Israel Adesanya, upsetting his spotless record of 20-0-0 fights until that fight. Blachowicz took out Reyes, another up-and-coming MMA prospect who was stopped short in September 2020.

Not only that but he fought and won against Ronaldo Souza and Luke Rockhold. He has been able to win most of those fights with a good mix of KOs and by Decision which means that Blachowicz is more than happy to go the distance with any fighter. That is precisely what Teixeira should be on the watch out for. Blachowicz is a deserved favorite in this one.

What about Teixeira Performing Well in the Bout?

We can definitely expect Teixeira to give it his all. He has 32-7-0 as his overall record and even though he is a bit older, Teixeira is every bit as formidable as his opponent. In fact, you may argue that Teixeira is unjustly posted as the underdog here, although there are subtle signs that betray that his age is working against him.

He has defeated Thiago Santos, Anthony Smith, Nikita Krylov, Ion Cutelaba and Karl Roberson in his last five bouts and his last loss was to Corey Anderson in 2018. Admittedly, Blachowicz was able to defeat Anderson.

Back in 2014, Teixeira was posted as the underdog to Jon Jones and he lost. There definitely seems to be fighting spirit left in the titan of MMA but so far as his chances of winning on Saturday go, we believe The Brazilian Pit Bull would be ultimately subdued by Blachowicz.

As to the rest of the fights, you can expect some interesting developments all over the place. We think Ankalaev will have no problem bringing home the fight against Oezdemir and Volkov should be able to win against Tybura.
