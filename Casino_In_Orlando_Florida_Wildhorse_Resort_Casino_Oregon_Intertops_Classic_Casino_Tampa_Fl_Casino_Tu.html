Gambling laws and regulations in Ireland

Ireland doesn't have strict gambling regulations and laws like the neighbouring United Kingdom, or Great Britain to be more specific, does. While the UK has the Gambling Commission which regulates and oversees all gambling-related activities concerning UK citizens and happening in the United Kingdom, Ireland currently doesn't have a regulatory body for gambling.

Any online casino that wants to offer services to Irish players needs to register with the country's Revenue Commissioner and pay taxes in Ireland. If these conditions are met, the gambling operator can operate in Ireland and/or offer its services to players from Ireland.

Irish players can gamble at any offshore online casino that holds a valid license from another European country (such as United Kingdom, Malta, Sweden, Germany, etc.), and most importantly meets the criteria mentioned above.

Note: Ireland does issue gambling licenses, but these apply just to bookmakers. All Irish bookmakers must hold an Irish Bookmaker's License. This makes sense, as sports betting is very popular in Ireland, but online casinos and their players are left out at the moment.

Although there currently isn't any Irish license that casino operators can apply for, this might change soon. Ireland plans to act similarly to many European countries and create its own gambling regulatory authority that would issue licenses to Irish online casino sites and other subjects that operate in the gambling sphere. If this happens, the selection of online casinos available to Irish players will shrink to those holding the official license. On the other hand, it's fair to argue that this change will most likely make the Irish gambling market easier to navigate and safer for players.

Gambling in Northern Ireland

Northern Ireland is a part of the United Kingdom, but not Great Britain. This puts it into a small legislative gap, as the UK Gambling Commission focuses on the British countries and Northern Ireland is a bit left out, as it's legislatively not a part of Ireland either.

Gambling in Northern Ireland is still regulated by an outdated law from 1985, which simply doesn't account for gambling over the internet, as we mentioned in our list of casinos for UK players.

The situation might change in the future, but at the moment, it's advisable for players from Northern Ireland who wish to gamble online to simply choose an online casino with a good reputation and a license from a respected regulator. Casinos licensed by the Gambling Commission are arguably the best choice because of the geographical and legislative proximity.
