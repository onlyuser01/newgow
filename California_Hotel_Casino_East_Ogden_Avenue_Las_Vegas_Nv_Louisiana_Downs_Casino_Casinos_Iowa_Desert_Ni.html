Shortened MLB Season Starts Thursday, ‘Extremely Tough’ to Handicap, Says BetMGM’s Main

Posted on: July 22, 2020, 10:33h.

Last updated on: July 23, 2020, 10:42h.

Major League Baseball finally gets underway Thursday, nearly four months after play was supposed to begin.

Social distancing wasn’t a thing yet when the Washington Nationals won the World Series last year in Houston. On Thursday, they host a team favored to win it this year, the New York Yankees, to start the 2020 MLB season. (Image: CBS Sports)

The Washington Nationals, the defending World Series champion, will host the New York Yankees, a favorite to win it all this year. They get the season started with a 7 p.m. ET game. The nightcap features another Series favorite, the Los Angeles Dodgers, who host the rival San Francisco Giants.

COVID-19 has drastically altered how the game will be played this year. For starters, the season lasts just 60 games instead of 162. In addition, we’ll now see (if you’re like me, thankfully) the designated hitter in the National League. We’ll also see runners on base to start extra innings in an attempt to keep teams from busting pitching staffs in extra-long affairs.

The new rules and uncertainty with COVID-19 will make this “an extremely tough” season to handicap, according to David Main, BetMGM’s head of US sports trading.

“I’m expecting there to be some fairly significant price movements on the games each day around player news,” Main told Casino.org.

The shortened pre-season presents its own challenges in getting players up to full fitness, and with the compacted season schedule and not much chance for players to recover between games, I suspect we will see a few players getting injured or rested each day, which will impact prices,” Main continued. “Not to mention that impact a positive COVID-19 test may have on a team, particularly if multiple players test positive.”

Bettors are flocking to the Yankees, who are the co-favorites with the Dodgers at +375 to win the World Series. BetMGM has written the most tickets on the Bronx Bombers, but the book has taken more money on Los Angeles. The Yankees have the second-highest handle, but the Tampa Bay Rays are third. Main said their initial odds of +3000 attracted early interest. The Rays are now available at +2000.

In terms of tickets written, after the Yanks, it’s the New York Mets, followed by the Dodgers, Minnesota Twins, Chicago Cubs, and the Chicago White Sox.
