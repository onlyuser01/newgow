Renewing the license in Sweden marks a strategic achievement for the company that will continue to provide its leading games for customers within the market

Online content supplier for the casino industry Play’n GO announced it renewed its license in Sweden. The company unveiled Wednesday that it was able to acquire a Swedish business-to-business (B2B) license, allowing it to continue to provide its content to audiences in the country.

Besides enabling Play’n GO to continue to deliver content in Sweden, the license strengthens the company’s presence in the European iGaming sector. This license is particularly important for Play’n GO considering that Sweden is the country where the company was founded.

As a result, customers in the country will be able to enjoy Play’n GO’s latest hit Legion Gold, its most popular online slot game called Book of Dead, as well as other titles within its continuously growing portfolio of content.

Sweden Remains an Important Market for the Company

Magnus Olsson, Play’n GO’s chief commercial officer (CMO), explained that Sweden is a home market for the country. He pointed out that although the company has a growing global presence, it still remains committed to offering its leading content to customers in Sweden. “It’s extra special for us to receive one of the first licenses from the regulator Spelinspektionen,” added Olsson.
