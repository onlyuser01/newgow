With the Prague Gaming & Tech Summit fast approaching, Hipther Agency, the event hosts, have revealed the title sponsors for the expo which will take place on March 29 and March 30 at Vienna House Andel’s Prague.

The event will bring together gaming and technology industry experts and offer them the opportunity to network and explore various aspects of the gaming industry. Hipther Agency has vowed to bring quality content, panel discussions and keynote addresses throughout the event.

Now, the agency has also revealed the names of the sponsors for the upcoming industry gathering, including Cloudflare, GR8 TECH, Nsoft, nanocosmos, Nordic Gambling, Endorphina, No Fluff Jobs, Vit2Go, Bragg Group, Tom Horn Gaming, and TriStratus. Commenting on this new announcement, Hipther Co-founder and Head of Business Zoltan Tuendik said:

"We have allocated a limited amount of sponsorship packages in order to provide the optimal visibility for all companies that have decided to support our event as sponsors."

Tuendik said that he was happy to see that all packages had been booked and that there were companies that have already expressed interest in backing next year’s edition of the event. Hipther Agency’s Prague Gaming & Tech Summit will come with a "full house," the press release shared by the company notes.

C-level executives, government officials and compliance experts involved in esports, blockchain, fintech, iGaming, artificial intelligence, virtual, augmented and mixed reality will all be in attendance. Prague Gaming & Tech Summit is a unique industry gathering that brings closely-related industries together and creates opportunities for cross-sector cooperation.

There will be a number of novelty technology pitches to explore, with metaverse experiences available, as well as traditional discussions about the nature of the gaming industry and how it is all evolving and faced with a new regulatory context. For their part, the sponsors were extremely pleased with the opportunity.

Bragg Gaming Head of Turnkey Solutions Matej Filipančič welcomed the collaboration. TriStratus Managing Partner Asif Akram called it a fantastic opportunity, and Tom Horn Gaming CEO Ondrej Lapides said the company was looking forward to meet leading industry experts on-site.
