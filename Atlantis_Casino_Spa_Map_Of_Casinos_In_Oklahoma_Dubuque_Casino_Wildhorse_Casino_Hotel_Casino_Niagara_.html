Sports Betting in Ohio to Launch by January 2023

The much-anticipated amended House Bill 29 legalizing sports betting in the Buckeye State passed through both legislative chambers last December and was officially sealed by Governor Mike DeWine’s signature. At the moment, the real money sports betting market in the state is now solely pending a regulatory framework that will establish the way casinos, sports teams, and racinos will obtain their licenses for mobile sports betting. Each of these categories of operators will be granted a single license, with the chance to get a second one provided they can prove their contribution can grow Ohio’s economy. The same legislation will also convert lottery kiosks and allow limited sports betting activities at bars, restaurants, and grocery stores. College sports betting is also going to be covered by the legislation once Ohio will open the market.
