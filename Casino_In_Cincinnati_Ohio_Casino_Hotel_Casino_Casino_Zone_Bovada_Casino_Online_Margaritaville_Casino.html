Jordan Going for Glory at Asian Cup

Reaching the semifinal stage of the Asian Cup meant breaking the national record for Jordan. This is the first time this team has reached the final four. Can they take it a step further?

They sure have the motivation to do so. However, their roster just doesn’t seem great. In fact, most of their players play in Jordan’s domestic league. There are a couple who play in other countries in the Middle East with their most-capped midfielder, Mousa Al-Tamari, being the only player who plays in Europe.

So, how did they get to the semifinal? Their schedule wasn’t too difficult, although they did have to play against some real heavyweights, most notably, South Korea. These two met in the group stage and the final score was 2-2.

It was a strange game, in which Jordan had a 2-1 lead until stoppage time when one of their players scored an own goal.

South Korea’s Asian Cup Games are Super-Fun to Watch

These guys have been very entertaining to watch since coming to Qatar. Not if you’re their fan, though. South Korea’s fans have had a lot of things to worry about throughout this tournament, with their boys making all kinds of mistakes that, luckily, did not prove costly for them.
