After a successful inaugural event, gaming and esports community PAX is headed for the second leg of the Ignition Series dedicated to VALORANT in North America.

PAX Hosts Second Event in VALORANT Ignition Series North America

PAX Arena Valorant Invitational is a significant event for the North American VALORANT crowd as it’s the second leg in the Riot’s global Ignition Series.

The whole intiative is part of Riot Games’ effort to reach out to third-parties and allow them to host esports tournaments for VALORANT, following specific pre-determined guidelines. In doing so, Riot hopes to encourage the grassroots and organic development of the esports ecosystem around the game.

Hosted and produced by established esports and gaming community PAX, VALORANT is off to a great start. The official sponsor of the event at the time is Seagate, which manufactures top-of-the-range SSD hard drives.

The event itself will feature a total prize pool worth $25,000 and the winning prize has been set at $10,000. The event itself will stretch for five days from July 22 through July 26.

How Are the VALORANT PAX Arena Ignition Series Played?

There will be two main formats for the event. The first one focuses on the group stages which will run in four groups of five teams each. The match-ups will be in a Best of Three (BO3) format and the top two teams of each team will progress to the playoffs.

Once in the playoffs, teams will switch the format to a single elimination bracket with no second chances for those who fail to make the cut. Quarterfinals will be played in a BO3 format whereas the Grand Finals will be played in a Best of Five (BO5) format instead.
