Newcastle to Start the 2022-23 Season in Top Gear

Since the 2021 Saudi takeover, Newcastle became the home to a number of new players. In January, the Magpies signed former England international Kieran Trippier, along with promising Brazilian midfielder Bruno Guimaraes, and a few others. At the time, they also appointed Eddie Howe as their new manager.

The revolution proved successful quickly afterward – Newcastle got away from the relegation zone, eventually finishing in the #11 position in the Prem.

This season, their ambitions are even high. No surprise there, as they’ve completed a few more amazing transfers during the summer transfer window. Goalkeeper Nick Pope came to Newcastle from Burnley, defender Sven Botman signed from Lille, while wing-back Matt Target inked a permanent deal after a six-month loan from Aston Villa.

Knowing all this, no one should be surprised that Newcastle is seen as favorite in the clash with Nottingham Forest, a team that’s playing its first Premier League game in the 21st century on Saturday.

Nottingham Forest Fired Up for Their First EPL Game

In fact, before qualifying for the Premier League through the Championship Playoffs last season, they had spent more than 20 years playing in the lower echelons of the English soccer league system.
