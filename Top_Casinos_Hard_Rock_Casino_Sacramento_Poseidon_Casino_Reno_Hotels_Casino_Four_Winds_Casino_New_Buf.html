CashtoCode: a discreet and secure service for UK customers

The concept was simple. Online merchants would be connected to real-world retail outlets where the customer could go and pay for the e-product or service. The key was the 'code', or the barcode, which the customer would get from the website on their mobile device to take to the outlet. It was a win-win: for the customer, who preferred the anonymity of cash, and the merchants who increased their consumer base.

The pros and cons of CashtoCode

CashtoCode's big selling point is the fact that users don't need to share any bank details: they just need cash in hand and a way of getting to the nearest store. It allows a degree of privacy that traditional card methods don't allow, with no transaction details appearing on a bank statement, and is also as secure as paying in cash.

It also offers an instant service, or at least however long it takes to get to the retail outlet. Once the user has the barcode on their mobile, or printout, then they simply need to get the attendant to scan it and the money arrives with the merchant instantly. This is potentially much quicker than a bank transfer and some card payments.

CashtoCode's payment limit, £250 at the time of writing in June 2021, could be a problem for people who wish to deposit large amounts, though. The service is also only available in certain countries, so if you're located outside of these then you'll have to choose another payment method, for now.
