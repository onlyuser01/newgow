Barcelona Desperate for a Win

Barcelona got to the Europa League after failing to qualify for the knockout phase of the Champions League. This team finished third in its UCL group, failing to advance to the playoffs for the first time in more than a decade.

Barca also ended its Copa Del Rey journey too soon. Despite being one of the favorites to win the Spanish Cup, Barca got knocked out of the competition by Athletic Bilbao in the Round of 16.

When it comes to Spanish La Liga, Barcelona is currently in second place, but with only a tiny chance of getting to the top. It’s because the table-leader Real Madrid has a significant point advantage.

What all this means is that practically the only way for Barcelona to win a trophy this season is to win the Europa League. But they’re still far away from the title. Their first step is to get past Eintracht, for which they need to win on Thursday.

Barcelona players have the motivation to win, but do they have the quality? Sure, they do – they’re one of the best squads on the continent. The trouble is that Eintracht isn’t too bad either.

Eintracht to Fight Back

The German team was pretty impressive in the first leg, making 16 goals attempts against Barcelona. In comparison, Barca’s players created just seven chances.

What’s even more impressive is that Eintracht played with one player fewer than Barcelona from the 78th minute until the ref’s final whistle. It’s because their defender Tuta got sent off for roughing one of Barca’s players.

Eintracht showed they got what it takes to keep up with Barca. However, this Thursday, they’re playing on the road. Although they’re decent in away games – 3 wins, 2 draws, and 1 loss in 2022 – Barca is even better when playing at home.
