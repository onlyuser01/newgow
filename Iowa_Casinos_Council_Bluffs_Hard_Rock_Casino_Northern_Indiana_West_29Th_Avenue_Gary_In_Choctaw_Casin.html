Many players get tempted by the odds. Now that you know Keno odds, you are probably thinking, well, here is my chance of really making a difference. Hold your horses as Keno remains a game of pure chance. There are no patterns in how the numbers are drawn.

The casinos use a Random Number Generator (RNG), and land-based casinos already have video Keno machines that draw numbers on a randomized principle. Now, a machine cannot really do a “random” outcome, but it uses a special algorithm that is unfathomable and, as such, allows it to produce outcomes that are unrelated.

To put it this way, unless you know the so-called “seed number,” your chances of winning aren’t going to improve. Because all drawn numbers are based on chance, you don’t have an inherent benefit in changing your numbers for each game you play.

Surely, you may still want to try a few different options, as replaying the same selection of picks will soon feel a little drab. Don’t worry about “missing out” on a number. There is no mathematical evidence that suggests you missed anything!
