Chile advanced a new online betting bill that will change how the vertical is regulated. The measure was first introduced in March last year but was stuck in limbo until now. While the bill will still need additional approvals, its advancement was praised by the gambling industry.

As of the time of this writing, the only regulated gambling companies in Chile are Lotería de Concepción, Polla Chilena de Beneficencia and Teletrak. Under new rules that were introduced in March, the Internal Revenue Service acquired the power to exclude offshore companies from the simplified tax regime, including sportsbooks, which are still unregulated.

At a later date, the Chamber of Deputies proposed to outlaw deals between betting companies and sportsbooks. For reference, the online sports betting market had already established a significant presence in local sports. The new bill, however, would regulate online betting, allowing online sportsbook companies to establish a foothold in Chile.

If Chile launches regulated online sports betting, it will likely hand out licenses through a non-competitive and administrative process. Bettors, on the other hand, will be assigned an ID before playing, making it easier to identify and prevent gambling harm.

Finally, Chile will only allow licensed companies to promote their products and will hand out fines to black-market brands that do not cease to market their products in the country.
