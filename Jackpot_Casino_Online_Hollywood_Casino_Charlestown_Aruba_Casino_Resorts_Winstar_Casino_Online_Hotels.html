Fontainebleau Las Vegas will celebrate its opening with a performance from the celebrity hip-hop singer Post Malone on December 30 and 31

Fontainebleau Las Vegas is a brand new Strip property set to welcome its first guests on Wednesday. While the casino hotel will offer premium gaming offerings, it will also double down on live entertainment and nightlife. Las Vegas Sun, a local news outlet, said that guests should be prepared for a “full and fresh Vegas resort experience.”

Set to open in a few days, Fontainebleau Las Vegas has been in development for some time now. The property boasts a stunning casino featuring a 150,000 square feet gaming area. Visitors will be able to play a variety of slots, table games and wager on sports. In the meantime, a VIP salon and a VIP rooftop experience will provide high-rollers with unprecedented thrill.

According to Fedor Banuchi, SVP of entertainment, special events and sponsorships, the Fontainebleau brand is “synonymous with entertainment, going back to the Rat Pack era.” Since the opening of the first Fontainebleau hotel in Miami in 1954, the brand has become a phenomenon loved by celebrities, high-rollers and regular players alike.

Fontainebleau LV Will Welcome Post Malone for a New Year Party

Banuchi believes that Fontainebleau’s commitment to entertainment is what sets it apart from other casino brands. In line with this philosophy, the Fontainebleau Las Vegas will celebrate its opening with a performance from the celebrity hip-hop singer Post Malone at the property’s 3,800-seat BleauLive Theater on December 30 and 31. Malone will also perform at Fontainebleau’s LIV club where he will be joined by Metro Boomin.

In addition to that, Fontainebleau will feature a variety of non-gaming venues designed to satisfy the needs of a variety of guests. For example, the Nowhere Lounge will offer live music every week. The LIV Nightclub, on the other hand, will provide nightlife lovers with a great place to party.

For reference, LIV is one of the best-known nightclub brands in the entire US. Its founder, David Grutman, opened a club at the Fontainebleau Miami Beach hotel in 2008 and is a longtime partner of the Fontainebleau brand. Grutman will also launch Papi Steak and Komodo restaurants at the casino hotel, further enhancing the overall experience provided by Fontainebleau Las Vegas. In the spring, he will also launch a LIV Beach pool club at the property.

Grutman concluded that he hopes for an explosive opening event with Fontainebleau Las Vegas.
