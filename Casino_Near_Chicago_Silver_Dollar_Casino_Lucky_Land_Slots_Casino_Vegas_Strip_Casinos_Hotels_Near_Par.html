Plainridge Park Casino to Cooperate in Investigation

Plainridge Park Casino has chosen PENN Entertainment as its sportsbook partner in Massachusetts, which sparked the concerns of the Massachusetts Gaming Commission when they held the license hearing on December 6. The Commission could not make a decision then and asked for further information and investigation.

The Commission’s considerations were regarding PENN Entertainment’s recent acquisition of Barstool Sports whose spokesperson and founder Dave Portnoy is very popular among younger audiences. The Commission voiced fears that the appeal of Portnoy will have a negative effect on the state’s efforts to control and fight gambling harm.

The Boston Herald reported that, during its second hearing on Tuesday, the Commission finally granted a temporary license to Plainridge Park Casino under the condition that the casino will cooperate with the Investigations and Enforcement Bureau in order to find out whether Portnoy’s presence could have a negative impact by attracting underage audiences. A further condition is that Plainridge has to ensure that nobody who is under 21 will have access to any of the Barstool College Football Shows.

The Commission’s decision was nudged in the right direction by PENN Entertainment president Jay Snowden who extensively elaborated that the company has been operating other Barstool branded sportsbooks in various US states and never had any of the mentioned issues.
