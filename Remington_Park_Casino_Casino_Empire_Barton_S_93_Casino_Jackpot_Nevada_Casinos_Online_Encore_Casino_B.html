Angel City Selects Yaamava’ as Official Casino Partner

Planned National Women’s Soccer League expansion team Angel City Football Club joined forces with Yaamava’ Resort and Casino at San Manuel, California. Under the new collaboration, the venue was named an official casino partner of the team. The collaboration will see the duo support youth of the Inland Empire region in Southern California by promoting physical activities and education. Moreover, the collaboration will see a series of soccer camps.

Considering that both Angel City and Yaamava’ are committed to strengthening their communities and share the same values, they will activate the Angel City Sponsorship Model. Yaamava’ explained that this innovative model is structured “for every team partner to make a charitable impact in the Inland Empire.” The new collaboration will bring a local Inland Empire Youth Team to an ACFC VIP game. The unique experience is expected to launch at the debut of Angel City during the 2022 season.

With a focus on the local schools in which most children qualify for free or reduced lunches, ACFC and Yaamava’ will deliver a unique combination of soccer training and personal development for all participants.” Statement released by Yaamava’ Resort and Casino

Last but not least, AC’s community coaches will run a soccer clinic for some 150 youth players between the age of seven and 14 in San Bernardino. Angel City, together with Yaamava’, will help all participants with personal development as well as soccer training. The club is also going to donate soccer equipment to a local sports program.

Yaamava’ Is Delighted to Team Up with ACFC

ACFC’s head of revenue, Jessica Smith, outlined that the club is proud to partner with Yaamava’. Additionally, she acknowledged that the announcement has been long anticipated and the duo now has a goal to make a positive impact within the vast region around Los Angeles. In conclusion, Smith added that the new partnership expands the “footprint of community impact to the Inland Empire, starting with our one-of-a-kind youth clinics.”

Yaamava’ Resort & Casino at San Manuel is delighted to announce our partnership with Angel City Football Club.” Rikki Tanenbaum, COO at the San Manuel Band of Mission Indians

San Manuel Band of Mission Indians’ chief operating officer Rikki Tanenbaum said that Yaamava’ Resort and Casino is delighted to join forces with Angel City FC. She acknowledged that the Tribe picks partners that share its values. According to Tanenbaum, the club’s mission to strengthen and empower the Inland Empire’s youth and communities is great and resonates deeply within the tribe.
