The technology-driven provider of innovative iGaming and iLottery solutions, NeoGames SA, released its unaudited results for the fourth quarter and 2022, outlining strong growth of revenue. The new trading update reveals that the total revenues and the company’s share in NPI revenues for 2022 hit $210.2 million. When compared to the $84.5 million reported for the year ended December 31, 2021, this result represented a significant increase of 149%.

NeoGames explained that the growth of the figure last year reflects “the business combination with Aspire Global.” At the same time, the company said that the total NPI revenues for 2022 surpassed even the high end of the projected $197 million to $208 million guidance for last year.

Not unexpectedly, the revenue reported by NeoGames for 2022 increased as well. Judging by the newly released report, the company has seen revenue of $165.7 million for 2022, representing a significant increase when compared to the $50.5 million reported for the corresponding period in 2021.

According to the new report, NeoGames’ net loss in 2022 was $19 million or negative $0.64 per share when compared to the result from the previous year which was a net income of $4.7 million or $0.18 per share. “The change in net loss during 2022 was mainly due to a $0.53 per share impact from the amortization of intangible assets related to the Aspire Global acquisition and acquisition-related expenses,” outlined the company in its trading update.

The new report revealed that the company’s total revenues and share in NPI revenues for the fourth quarter last year was $83.2 million. This result marked another significant increase when compared to the corresponding period in 2021 when the figure was $21.3 million.

“We significantly evolved our business through our acquisition of Aspire Global, expanding beyond our position as a leader in global iLottery solutions, and propelling us to becoming a global leader in providing technology and content solutions across iLottery, online sport betting and iGaming.“ Moti Malul, CEO of NeoGames
