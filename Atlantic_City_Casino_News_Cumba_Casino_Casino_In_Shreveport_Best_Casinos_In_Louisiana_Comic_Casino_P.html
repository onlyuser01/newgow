Twin River and PointsBet Signed a Multi-Year Partnership

The sports betting operator PointsBet signed a multi-year partnership with Twin River Worldwide Holdings earlier this week. Under the new partnership PointsBet will access the New Jersey gaming market by launching online gambling and casino platform. The agreement outlines that PointsBet will be paying Twin River a percentage of the net gaming revenue.

In addition, the sports betting operator is going to be responsible for securing of a license as well as the costs for launching of its online platform and mobile application. Prior to launching online gaming and casino services, PointsBet will need to receive approval from the New Jersey’s regulator.

The finalization of the partnership was contingent on the acquisition of Bally’s Atlantic City Hotel and Casino by Twin River. Although the acquisition deal for $25 million was struck back in April, it had to wait for the megamerger of Eldorado-Caesars. Since the merger was completed last month, now the acquisition is awaiting the approval of the state’s regulators. Looking back in July, Twin Rivers struck another key deal aiming at acquiring Eldorado Resorts’ subsidiary companies which own the Isle Kansas City in Missouri and the Lady Luck casinos in Mississippi.

Here is what Twin River Worldwide Holdings president and CEO, George Papanier said about the partnership: “PointsBet will be a great addition to our growing partnerships with innovative leaders around the world.” He continued by saying that the company is extremely excited to have the opportunity to participate in the best mobile gaming environment, created by New Jersey. Papanier concluded by saying that the company will continue to bring innovative offers to the growing market.
