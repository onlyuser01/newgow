Online gambling in Austria

Legal gambling in Austria is centred around the Glücksspielgesetz (GSpG) which the government passed in 1989. This was the first time that the casino and lottery operators needed formal licenses to operate, thus legally recognizing and regulating the industry in one stroke.

When the internet brought online casino and lottery games with it a decade or so later, Austria simply amended the GSpG to recognize online gambling as a legal activity.

But there was a catch. The amendment only allowed for the creation of 15 Austrian online casino licenses, with companies needing to pass strict criteria to get one. The biggest operator, Casinos Austria, got 12 of them within its network of companies, while three other operators obtained one each. Later, these three rivals all lost their licenses for failing to follow Austrian laws, meaning Casinos Austria had a legal monopoly that still exists today.

While this can be seen as bad news for Austrian players in theory (as fewer operators means less choice and a potential dip in standards), the system seems to work today for two reasons.

Players have a range of regulated domestic operators to choose from, albeit under the influence of the same company An EU directive forces Austria to allow residents to access foreign online gambling sites.

The evolution of online gambling in recent years has put pressure on the government to form a new regulatory framework. This would include a self-exclusion program for online players, a blacklist of gambling sites, control of harmful loot boxes, and stringent digital anti-corruption measures. However, we have yet to see the authorities give this the official 'go-ahead', meaning Austria still has largely the same online gambling regulation as at the start of the 2000s.
