Kansspelautoriteit (KSA)

The Kansspelautoriteit, or Dutch Gaming Authority, was formed on 1st April 2012 with three core objectives - to protect consumers, to prevent gambling addiction, and to combat illegal practices. It is an independent body which implements and enforces the Dutch Gambling Act 2021 (KOA) on behalf of the Minister for Legal Protection. In pursuit of perfection The KSA states that it "strives for a world in which games of chance are reliable, providers are transparent and players are protected" - a noble cause indeed. To achieve this the authority has prescribed itself several statutory tasks: To regulate the market - by granting licenses and ensuring that the conditions of those licenses are met

To supervise - by making sure that all laws and regulations are followed to the letter

To enforce - by taking action against any illegal gambling activity

To prevent addiction - by ensuring licensees are implementing robust controls and promoting awareness of gambling risks

To inform and educate - by making available information to players and operators and answering any questions

To combat match-fixing among sports betting license holders

To provide a way for players to exclude themselves from gambling By the application of tight measures and strict controls, the KSA can be certain that only reputable companies can operate in the Dutch market.

Complaints The KSA states that if players encounter problems with an operator such as operating without a license, not paying winnings, allowing underage gambling or partaking in fraud, they should report this to the authority. The authority will use such reports to determine if further investigation is warranted. However, it will not mediate disputes between players and operators, and recommends players to: Discuss the matter with the operator directly

Contact a complaints officer or a legal advisor

Report fraud to the police Once the report has been made, the player will only hear from the authority if more information is required. The results of any investigations based upon player reports can be viewed on the Your report, our supervision page.
