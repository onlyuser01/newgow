History of ecoCard

ecoCard was launched in the year 2000 by UK company PSI-Pay Ltd. Its intention was to explore the new digital market, and its name appealed to the growing interest in green business practices at the time.

In 2008, ecoCard was authorized by the FSA (Financial Services Authority) as an electronic money issuer â€“ a move that boosted their reputation in the UK payments industry. Its range of services expanded, too. Instead of just a prepaid card, the company began to offer a digital wallet and an app. This led to a rebranding in 2013: ecoPayz became the umbrella name for all company services, with the ecoCard being just one of them.

Note: The company has since enjoyed global success. It has created a presence in many countries, and forged a key partnership with Western Union, making them the only UK-based e-wallet to have such a deal. Another important link-up with Mastercard led to the ecoCard becoming the ecoPayz Mastercard: all prepaid cards now featured the famous Mastercard logo. Users with the old ecoCards had until the end of 2020 to use them before switching over to the new card.
