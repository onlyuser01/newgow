Golden State Warriors Fired Up to Get to the Playoffs

The Warriors are .455 (20-24) in the 2023-24 NBA, which puts them below the playoff zone. However, there are plenty of games left to be played before the end of the regular season, meaning that they still have a good chance of advancing to the postseason.

For that, however, they need to win games like these. The good thing is that they seem to have the motivation to pull it off. They’re playing in honor of their late assistant coach Dejan Milojevic, as well as to avoid the embarrassment of missing the playoffs for only the third time since head coach Steve Kerr took charge of the team in 2014.

The Warriors haven’t been in great form since the start of 2024, but they did put on a decent performance in their most recent game. They defeated the mighty Philadelphia 76ers whose best player Joel Embiid spent roughly half an hour on the court, scoring 14 points.

The man who scored the most points in that game was Steph Curry with 37 points. Before that, against their divisional rivals, the LA Lakers, he scored even more (46 points).

The Grizzlies have also had a very rough schedule lately, with their most recent game happening less than 24 hours before the clash with the Warriors.
