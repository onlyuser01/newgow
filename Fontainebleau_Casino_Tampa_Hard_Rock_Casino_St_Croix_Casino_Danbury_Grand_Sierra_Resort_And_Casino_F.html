El Mariachi Slot Review

El Mariachi slot game is not shy about where it took inspiration from. The legendary movie Desperado starring Antonio Banderas and Salma Hayek has found a new outlet in this creative slot game by Dragon Gaming. In the game, you will set out to explore the world of Mexican gunmen, a charming femme fatale, and a bonus round that is filled with multipliers, bonus features, sticky wilds, and re-spins.

A highly thematic experience from head to toe, El Mariachi will play easily, accommodate players of various bankrolls, and prove a truly outstanding and rewarding game from the moment you join it. Progressive bonus levels and decent payouts make El Mariachi slot game one of the best you will play today.

Theme, Storyline, and Graphics: Desperado Written All Over

El Mariachi slot has taken its cue from Desperado, the iconic movie starring Antonio Banderas and Salma Hayek in which El Mariachi, a famed gunslinger and guitarist portrayed by Banderas, returns home to a small border town in Mexico. The plot quickly spirals out of control when El Mariachi’s lover is murdered by a local drug lord, setting the man on a revenge quest.

The slot game is a little less gory than the flick, though, although the title is still designed to give you Mexican vibes, with the national flag waving softly in the air, and the setting of the game taking place in a bar – just the right spot for a Mexican standoff where guns are drawn, and nerves jarred.

You will be welcomed to a neatly designed game featuring a five-by-three grid. The spin button is a revolver’s cylinder, with an autoplay feature neatly tucked away. The low-paying symbols are designed as card suits, and the high-paying ones as characters from the movie. We will touch on the symbols in a moment.

El Mariachi Game Symbols and Payouts

El Mariachi slot doesn’t have too many symbols you will have to worry about, and they are all straightforward to read. The low-paying ones are shaped like card suits, meaning diamonds, clubs, hearts, and spades, and they will pay a fair bit based on how many you line up on the reels.

The high-paying symbols are the ones that depict characters from the movie itself, including El Mariachi, The Lady with the Rose, and the loathed Drug Lord who is seen as an antagonist of the slot and the show. Depending on which symbols you get, the payout will vary but there are some pretty nice ones.

The symbols pay from left to right, and Five Lady with the Rose would mean a payout of $1,000, and El Mariachi will pay just $300 for five. The Drug Lord himself will fetch you a $450 payline, whereas the other symbols, including the card suits, will pay $150 for five of each. These symbols are designed to be generous and fit both thematically and in terms of gameplay value.
