Inverse Martingale or Paroli

Another variation of a popular roulette strategy is the Inverse Martingale or Paroli. Here, you would want to double your bet after every win, which statistically makes it slightly safer than the traditional system.

The system is once again best tailored to outside bets, as you need the highest possible statistical chance to win, which is nearly 50% with most outside bets. Now, some versions of the game, such as American, may have a slightly steeper edge, so consider this as well.

D’Alembert

No roulette strategies guide would be quite complete without referencing to D’Alembert. D’Alembert is another progressive stratagem, and only this time players will not pursue aggressive stake hikes.

Instead, you bet in flat amounts. The first step is to pick a betting unit. Usually, around 0.50% of your total bankroll as long as that fits the minimum betting limit. In this strategy, you want to increase or decrease your bet size by one unit depending on whether you have won or lost. Here is an example:

So long as you have money left, you are not likely to ever hit the table limit, which means you can take D’Alembert to low-limit tables, too!

You may be convinced that some numbers are definitely better than others when it comes to roulette. Yet, there is absolutely no evidence to support this. If you took 10,000 spins of the wheel, you should get a perfectly asymmetrical result, with no specific number favored heavily over another.
